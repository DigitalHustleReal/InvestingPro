## Core Voice Principles

### 1. Expert but Approachable

**DO:**
- Use financial terms correctly, but explain them naturally
- Show expertise through insights, not jargon
- Write like a knowledgeable friend, not a professor

**Example:**
✅ "The HDFC Regalia offers 4 reward points per ₹150 spent, which translates to roughly 2.7% value back on your spending."

❌ "The HDFC Regalia provides a reward accrual rate of 4 points per ₹150, yielding an effective reward rate of 2.7%."

### 2. Indian-First

**DO:**
- Use Indian currency (₹) and Indian contexts
- Reference Indian banks, Indian regulations (SEBI, RBI)
- Use Indian examples (e.g., "If you spend ₹20,000/month on groceries")
- Understand Indian financial behavior and concerns

**Example:**
✅ "If you spend ₹15,000/month on fuel, the ICICI HPCL Super Saver card can save you ₹1,800/year in fuel surcharge waivers."

❌ "If you spend $200/month on fuel, you can save money with fuel cards."

### 3. No BS

**DO:**
- Be direct and honest
- Call out fees, limitations, and downsides
- Don't oversell or exaggerate
- Acknowledge when something isn't the best fit

**Example:**
✅ "The Axis Bank Magnus card offers excellent rewards, but the ₹10,000 annual fee only makes sense if you spend ₹1 lakh+ per month."

❌ "The Axis Bank Magnus is the perfect card for everyone with amazing rewards!"

## Tone Guidelines

### Primary Tone: Confident Helper

- **Confident:** We know our stuff, and we show it
- **Helpful:** We're here to solve problems, not show off
- **Respectful:** We respect our users' intelligence and time
- **Direct:** We get to the point quickly

### Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Product Comparison** | Analytical, objective | "The HDFC Regalia scores 8.5/10 for travel rewards, while the Axis Magnus scores 9/10 but requires higher spending." |
| **Decision Guidance** | Supportive, clear | "If you spend ₹20K/month on groceries, here are the 3 best cards that maximize your rewards." |
| **Explaining Concepts** | Simple, practical | "SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) is like a recurring deposit, but for mutual funds. You invest a fixed amount monthly." |
| **Warnings/Disclaimers** | Serious, clear | "Mutual fund investments are subject to market risks. Past performance doesn't guarantee future returns." |

## Language & Style

### Writing Style

1. **Active Voice Preferred**
   - ✅ "We analyzed 50 credit cards"
   - ❌ "50 credit cards were analyzed by us"

2. **Short Sentences**
   - ✅ "This card offers 5% cashback. It's perfect for online shopping."
   - ❌ "This card, which offers 5% cashback, is perfect for online shopping because it maximizes rewards on digital transactions."

3. **Bullet Points for Lists**
   - ✅ Use bullets for features, pros, cons
   - ❌ Long paragraphs for lists

4. **Numbers Over Words**
   - ✅ "₹50,000" not "fifty thousand rupees"
   - ✅ "5% cashback" not "five percent cashback"

### Indian English Conventions

- Use "lakh" and "crore" (₹5 lakh, ₹10 crore)
- Use Indian date format when relevant (DD/MM/YYYY)
- Use Indian bank names and terms (e.g., "current account", "savings account")
- Reference Indian regulations (SEBI, RBI, IRDAI)

## Decision-Focused Writing

### The Decision Framework

Every piece of content should follow: **Problem → Compare → Decide → Apply**

#### 1. Problem (Identify the Need)
- Start with the user's situation
- Example: "If you spend ₹20,000/month on groceries..."

#### 2. Compare (Show Options)
- Present 3-5 options with clear differences
- Use comparison tables when possible
- Highlight key differentiators

#### 3. Decide (Recommend)
- Give clear recommendations with reasoning
- Explain why this option fits their situation
- Acknowledge trade-offs

#### 4. Apply (Action)
- Provide clear next steps
- Include affiliate links for monetization
- Make application easy

### Content Types (Priority Order)

1. **Decision-Focused Articles** (HIGHEST PRIORITY)
   - "Best credit card for ₹20K/month grocery spending"
   - "Best mutual fund SIP for ₹5000/month"
   - "Best loan for ₹10 lakh home purchase"

2. **Comparison Articles** (HIGH PRIORITY)
   - "HDFC Regalia vs Axis Magnus: Which is Better?"
   - "Top 5 ELSS Funds for Tax Saving 2026"

3. **How-To Guides** (MEDIUM PRIORITY - Only if decision-focused)
   - "How to Choose the Right Credit Card for Your Spending"
   - "How to Start a SIP: Step-by-Step Guide"

4. **Educational Content** (LOW PRIORITY - Remove or minimize)
   - ❌ "What is a Credit Card?"
   - ❌ "What is SIP?"
   - ✅ Replace with: "Best Credit Cards for Beginners" or "Best SIP Plans for ₹5000/month"

## Category-Specific Guidelines

### Credit Cards

**Voice:** Practical, reward-focused, honest about fees

**Key Elements:**
- Lead with rewards/benefits
- Be upfront about fees
- Explain eligibility clearly
- Show real-world value (e.g., "Save ₹2,400/year on fuel")

**Example Structure:**
1. Who this card is for
2. Key benefits (rewards, cashback, features)
3. Fees and charges
4. Eligibility requirements
5. How to apply

### Mutual Funds

**Voice:** Educational but practical, risk-aware, goal-oriented

**Key Elements:**
- Connect to goals (retirement, child education, etc.)
- Explain risk clearly
- Show SIP amounts and projected returns
- Include SEBI disclaimers

**Example Structure:**
1. Goal this fund helps achieve
2. Fund performance (with disclaimers)
3. Risk level and suitability
4. SIP recommendation
5. How to invest

### Loans

**Voice:** Transparent, cost-focused, eligibility-aware

**Key Elements:**
- Lead with interest rates
- Show total cost (principal + interest)
- Explain eligibility clearly
- Compare options

**Example Structure:**
1. Loan amount and purpose
2. Interest rates and total cost
3. Eligibility requirements
4. Comparison with alternatives
5. How to apply

## What to Avoid

### ❌ Educational-Only Content

**Don't Write:**
- "What is a Credit Card?"
- "What is SIP?"
- "Understanding Mutual Funds"

**Write Instead:**
- "Best Credit Cards for Beginners in India 2026"
- "Best SIP Plans for ₹5000/month Investment"
- "Best Mutual Funds for Retirement Planning"

### ❌ Generic Claims

**Don't Write:**
- "The best credit card"
- "Perfect for everyone"
- "Guaranteed returns"

**Write Instead:**
- "Best credit card for ₹20K/month grocery spending"
- "Ideal for frequent travelers spending ₹30K+/month"
- "Historical returns of 12% (past performance doesn't guarantee future returns)"

### ❌ Jargon Without Explanation

**Don't Write:**
- "The card offers 4X reward points on select MCCs"

**Write Instead:**
- "The card offers 4X reward points on dining and travel (restaurants, hotels, flights)"

### ❌ Overselling

**Don't Write:**
- "This is the ultimate credit card!"
- "You must get this card now!"

**Write Instead:**
- "This card offers excellent value if you spend ₹50K+/month"
- "Consider this card if you're a frequent traveler"

### ❌ Ignoring Indian Context

**Don't Write:**
- "$500 annual fee"
- "Best for US travelers"

**Write Instead:**
- "₹5,000 annual fee"
- "Best for international travel from India"

## Examples: Good vs Bad

### Example 1: Credit Card Article

**❌ BAD (Educational, Generic):**
> "Credit cards are financial instruments that allow you to make purchases on credit. They offer various benefits like rewards and cashback. This article explains what credit cards are and how they work."

**✅ GOOD (Decision-Focused):**
> "If you spend ₹20,000/month on groceries, the HDFC Regalia card can earn you ₹6,000/year in rewards. Here's how to maximize your grocery spending rewards and the 3 best cards for Indian grocery shoppers."

### Example 2: Mutual Fund Article

**❌ BAD (Educational, Generic):**
> "SIP stands for Systematic Investment Plan. It allows you to invest a fixed amount regularly in mutual funds. This article explains what SIP is and its benefits."

**✅ GOOD (Decision-Focused):**
> "Starting a ₹5,000/month SIP in the right mutual fund can grow to ₹10 lakh in 10 years. Here are the 5 best SIP plans for ₹5,000/month investment in 2026, with expected returns and risk levels."

### Example 3: Product Description

**❌ BAD (Overselling):**
> "The HDFC Regalia is the best credit card ever! It offers amazing rewards and is perfect for everyone!"

**✅ GOOD (Honest, Specific):**
> "The HDFC Regalia offers 4 reward points per ₹150 spent, translating to 2.7% value back. With a ₹2,500 annual fee (waived on ₹3 lakh annual spending), it's best for those spending ₹25K+/month. Ideal for frequent travelers and online shoppers."
