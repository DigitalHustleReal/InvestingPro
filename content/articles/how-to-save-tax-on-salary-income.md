# How to Save Tax on Salary Income — 10 Legal Ways for 2026

If you are a salaried employee in the 20% or 30% tax bracket, you could be paying Rs 1-3 lakh more in tax than necessary. The Income Tax Act provides numerous legitimate ways to reduce your taxable income — but most people either don't know about them or fail to plan early enough.

This guide covers 10 proven methods to legally reduce your tax burden as a salaried professional in India. All strategies apply to the **old tax regime**. If you are unsure which regime benefits you, start by reading our [old vs new tax regime comparison](/articles/old-vs-new-tax-regime-calculator).

## Key Takeaways

- A well-planned tax strategy can save Rs 1.5-3.5 lakh in tax annually for someone in the 30% bracket
- Start planning in April, not January-March
- HRA, home loan interest, and 80C are the three biggest deduction buckets
- The old regime allows more deductions; the new regime has lower base rates
- All strategies here are fully legal — tax planning is not tax evasion

## 1. Maximize Section 80C — Rs 1,50,000 Deduction

This is the most popular tax-saving section. You can invest up to Rs 1,50,000 in specified instruments:

**Best options (ranked by return potential):**
1. [ELSS mutual funds](/articles/best-elss-tax-saving-mutual-funds-2026) — 12-18% returns, 3-year lock-in
2. [PPF](/articles/complete-guide-ppf-public-provident-fund) — 7.1% tax-free, 15-year lock-in
3. EPF — 8.25% (auto-deducted from salary)
4. NPS — 8-12% market-linked returns

**Tax saving:** Rs 1,50,000 x 30% = Rs 45,000 + cess = Rs 46,800

Remember: Your EPF contribution already counts towards 80C. A Rs 80,000 salary has Rs 9,600/month EPF = Rs 1,15,200/year of 80C already used. Only Rs 34,800 remains for other investments.

For a complete breakdown, read our [Section 80C guide](/articles/section-80c-tax-saving-guide).

## 2. Claim HRA Exemption — Rs 2-4 Lakh for Metro Residents

If you live in rented accommodation and receive HRA (House Rent Allowance) as part of your salary, you can claim HRA exemption. This is one of the largest deductions available to salaried employees.

**HRA exemption is the lowest of:**
- Actual HRA received
- 50% of basic salary (metro cities) or 40% (non-metro)
- Actual rent paid minus 10% of basic salary

**Example:**
- Basic salary: Rs 8,00,000/year
- HRA received: Rs 4,00,000/year
- Rent paid: Rs 30,000/month = Rs 3,60,000/year
- City: Mumbai (metro)

Calculation:
- Actual HRA: Rs 4,00,000
- 50% of basic: Rs 4,00,000
- Rent minus 10% basic: Rs 3,60,000 - Rs 80,000 = Rs 2,80,000

**HRA exemption: Rs 2,80,000** (lowest of the three)

**Tax saving:** Rs 2,80,000 x 30% x 1.04 = Rs 87,360

### If You Don't Receive HRA
If your employer doesn't pay HRA (government employees, some private employees), you can claim deduction under Section 80GG:
- Maximum: Rs 60,000/year (Rs 5,000/month)
- Conditions: You, your spouse, or minor child should not own a house in the city of employment

### Documentation Required
- Rent receipts (monthly)
- Rental agreement
- Landlord's PAN (if rent exceeds Rs 1 lakh/year)
- Bank transfer proof for rent payments

## 3. Home Loan Interest — Rs 2,00,000 Under Section 24(b)

If you have a home loan on a self-occupied property, you can deduct up to Rs 2,00,000 of interest paid per financial year under Section 24(b).

**Additional:** The principal repayment qualifies under [Section 80C](/articles/section-80c-tax-saving-guide) (within the Rs 1.5 lakh limit).

**Tax saving on interest:** Rs 2,00,000 x 30% x 1.04 = Rs 62,400

**Note:** If the property is let out (rented), there is no limit on interest deduction, but it creates rental income that's taxable.

For home loan planning, see our [EMI calculator guide](/articles/home-loan-emi-calculator-guide) and [best home loan rates](/articles/best-home-loan-interest-rates-india-2026).

## 4. NPS Contribution — Additional Rs 50,000 Under 80CCD(1B)

The National Pension System offers an **extra Rs 50,000 deduction** under Section 80CCD(1B), over and above the Rs 1.5 lakh 80C limit.

**Tax saving:** Rs 50,000 x 30% x 1.04 = Rs 15,600

**Bonus:** If your employer offers NPS as a salary component, the employer's contribution (up to 14% of basic for government, 10% for others) is deductible under 80CCD(2) — this has no limit and is available in **both** old and new tax regimes.

Read our detailed [NPS tax benefits guide](/articles/nps-tax-benefits-section-80ccd).

## 5. Health Insurance Premium — Rs 25,000 to Rs 1,00,000 Under 80D

Section 80D allows deduction for health insurance premiums:

| Who | Below 60 | 60 or Above |
|-----|----------|-------------|
| Self, spouse, children | Rs 25,000 | Rs 50,000 |
| Parents | Rs 25,000 | Rs 50,000 |
| **Maximum total** | **Rs 50,000** | **Rs 1,00,000** |

**Additional:** Preventive health check-up of Rs 5,000 is included within the above limits.

**Example:** You (35) pay Rs 20,000 for self + family health insurance and Rs 35,000 for senior citizen parents' insurance.
**Total 80D deduction:** Rs 55,000
**Tax saving:** Rs 55,000 x 30% x 1.04 = Rs 17,160

Even without the tax benefit, [health insurance](/articles/best-health-insurance-plans-india-2026) is essential. The tax deduction is a bonus.

## 6. Education Loan Interest — Unlimited Under Section 80E

If you or your child has an education loan, the **entire interest paid** is deductible under Section 80E. There is no upper limit.

- Available for 8 years from the year you start repaying
- Applies to loans from banks and approved financial institutions
- Available for full-time courses in India or abroad

For someone paying Rs 2 lakh/year in education loan interest in the 30% bracket, this saves Rs 62,400 annually.

Read our [education loan guide](/articles/education-loan-guide-indian-students) for more details.

## 7. Leave Travel Allowance (LTA) Exemption

If your salary includes LTA, you can claim exemption for domestic travel expenses:

- **What's covered:** Economy class airfare or AC first-class rail fare for travel within India
- **Who's covered:** Self, spouse, children (max 2), dependent parents
- **Frequency:** Twice in a block of 4 years (current block: 2022-2025)
- **Documentation:** Travel tickets and boarding passes

**Tip:** Plan a domestic trip and claim LTA before the 4-year block ends to avoid losing the benefit.

## 8. Optimize Salary Structure

Work with your HR team to restructure your salary for maximum tax efficiency:

### Tax-Friendly Components to Request
1. **Higher HRA allocation** (if renting) — increases HRA exemption
2. **Food coupons/meal cards** — Rs 50 per meal tax-free (up to ~Rs 26,400/year)
3. **NPS employer contribution** — tax-free up to 10-14% of basic salary
4. **Car lease or conveyance allowance** — tax benefits on official use
5. **Telephone/internet reimbursement** — tax-free if for office use
6. **Leave encashment optimization** — tax-free up to Rs 25 lakh on retirement

### Components to Minimize
- **Special allowance** — fully taxable
- **Performance bonus** — fully taxable (consider deferring if possible)

**Important:** Salary restructuring must be done at the start of the financial year or at the time of joining. Mid-year changes are rarely possible.

## 9. Donations Under Section 80G

Donations to approved charitable organizations qualify for deduction:

- **100% deduction (no limit):** PM National Relief Fund, National Defence Fund, approved state funds
- **100% deduction (with limit):** Approved local authorities for family planning
- **50% deduction (with limit):** Other approved charities, temples, educational institutions

**Limit:** Deductions under 80G cannot exceed 10% of adjusted gross total income for most donations.

**Tip:** Ensure the organization has valid 80G registration. Get a receipt with the organization's PAN and 80G registration number.

## 10. Claim Standard Deduction

Both regimes offer a flat standard deduction — no proof or investment needed:

- **Old regime:** Rs 50,000
- **New regime:** Rs 75,000

This is automatically applied. In the old regime, this stacks with all other deductions.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Waiting till March:** Last-minute investing leads to poor choices (expensive insurance policies, unnecessary FDs)
2. **Not claiming HRA:** Many employees miss HRA because they don't collect rent receipts
3. **Paying landlord in cash:** Always pay rent via bank transfer for clean documentation
4. **Missing NPS 80CCD(1B):** The extra Rs 50,000 beyond 80C is often overlooked
5. **Not considering both regimes:** Run the numbers for both old and new regime every year — your optimal choice may change

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I claim both HRA and home loan deduction?

Yes, if you are paying rent in one city (where you work) and paying home loan EMI for a property in another city (hometown or investment property). You can claim HRA exemption and home loan interest deduction simultaneously.

### Is tax planning legal?

Absolutely. Tax planning (using legal provisions to minimize tax) is completely different from tax evasion (hiding income or fabricating deductions). Every deduction in this guide is explicitly provided by the Income Tax Act.

### What if I forgot to invest by March 31?

You cannot claim 80C deduction for investments made after March 31 of the financial year. However, you can file an updated return (within 24 months) if you made a genuine error in claiming deductions.

### How much can I save if I earn Rs 15 lakh?

With optimized deductions of Rs 5-6 lakh (HRA + 80C + 80D + NPS), you can save Rs 1.5-2 lakh in tax annually under the old regime. The exact amount depends on your specific deductions.

### Should I take a home loan just for tax saving?

No. The tax benefit is a bonus, not a reason to take a loan. A Rs 50 lakh home loan at 8.50% costs Rs 4.25 lakh/year in interest, but saves only Rs 62,400 in tax (on Rs 2 lakh deduction). You're still paying Rs 3.63 lakh net interest. Only buy a home when financially ready.
