# Direct vs Regular Mutual Funds — How to Choose and Save More

Every mutual fund in India is available in two variants: Direct and Regular. The underlying portfolio is identical — same stocks, same fund manager, same strategy. The only difference is who gets a commission — and that difference can cost you lakhs over your investment lifetime.

## Key Takeaways

- Direct plans have 0.5-1.5% lower expense ratios than regular plans
- Over 20 years, this difference can mean 15-25% more in your final corpus
- Regular plans pay commissions to distributors/advisors from your returns
- Direct plans are available on AMC websites, MF Central, Kuvera, Groww, and Zerodha Coin
- Switching from regular to direct is possible but triggers capital gains tax

## What's the Difference?

### Regular Plan
When you invest through a bank, distributor, or financial advisor, you are put into the "Regular" plan. The mutual fund company (AMC) pays your advisor a commission from the fund's expense ratio. This commission typically ranges from 0.5% to 1.5% per year of your invested amount.

You don't pay the advisor directly — the commission is deducted from the fund's NAV, so you see lower returns compared to the direct plan.

### Direct Plan
When you invest directly with the AMC or through a direct-plan platform (Kuvera, Groww, Zerodha Coin, MF Central), there is no intermediary commission. The expense ratio is lower, and the NAV is higher — you keep more of the fund's returns.

## Why Do Regular Plans Still Exist?

### Legitimate Reasons
1. **Financial advisors need compensation:** Registered Investment Advisors (RIAs) who charge fees can use direct plans, but distributors who give "free" advice are compensated through regular plan commissions.
2. **Hand-holding for beginners:** Some investors need an advisor to guide them through market crashes, goal planning, and portfolio rebalancing. The commission pays for this service.
3. **MFD livelihoods:** Millions of Mutual Fund Distributors (MFDs) across India depend on trail commissions for their income. They provide financial access in tier 2-3 cities.

### When Regular Plans Are Acceptable
- You genuinely need ongoing advisory and cannot manage investments yourself
- Your advisor is providing real value (tax planning, goal mapping, behavioral coaching)
- You have very small amounts (Rs 500-2,000/month) where the absolute difference is minimal

### When Regular Plans Are Not Justified
- Your bank relationship manager "suggests" funds during a branch visit (they earn commission)
- You're financially literate enough to read this article and make informed decisions
- An online platform defaults you to regular plans without clear disclosure
- The "advisor" only recommends NFOs or high-commission thematic funds

## How to Invest in Direct Plans

### Option 1: AMC Websites Directly
Visit the fund house website (e.g., miraeassetmf.co.in, iciciprumf.com) and create an account. You can start SIPs and make lumpsum investments directly.

**Pros:** No third-party dependency
**Cons:** Separate login for each AMC, no unified portfolio view

### Option 2: MF Central (Industry Platform)
MF Central (mfcentral.com) is run by CAMS and KFintech — the two mutual fund registrars. It provides a single platform to invest in direct plans across all AMCs.

**Pros:** Official, free, covers all AMCs
**Cons:** Interface is functional but not modern

### Option 3: Direct-Plan Platforms
- **Kuvera:** Free, no commissions, excellent portfolio analytics
- **Groww:** Free direct plans, clean interface, also offers stocks
- **Zerodha Coin:** Direct plans only, integrated with Zerodha demat
- **Paytm Money:** Direct plans with advisory features

**Pros:** Best user experience, portfolio tracking, goal planning
**Cons:** Platform risk (your investments are still with the AMC, so this is minimal)

## How to Switch from Regular to Direct

If you're currently in regular plans, here's how to switch:

### Step 1: Check Your Current Holdings
Log into MF Central or your AMC account to see which funds are in regular plans.

### Step 2: Understand Tax Implications
Switching from regular to direct is treated as a redemption + fresh purchase:
- **Equity funds (held 12+ months):** LTCG tax of 12.5% on gains above Rs 1.25 lakh
- **Equity funds (held <12 months):** STCG tax of 20%
- **Debt funds:** Taxed at your income tax slab rate

### Step 3: Switch Strategically
- **If gains are small:** Switch immediately — the tax hit is negligible
- **If gains are large:** Switch in batches across financial years to utilize the Rs 1.25 lakh LTCG exemption each year
- **For ongoing SIPs:** Simply stop the regular plan SIP and start a new SIP in the direct plan. Don't redeem existing regular plan units if there's significant taxable gain

### Step 4: Submit Switch Request
You can submit a switch request through:
- The AMC website/app
- MF Central
- Direct-plan platforms (if your folio is linked)

## Direct Plans and ELSS Tax Savings

For [ELSS tax-saving funds](/articles/best-elss-tax-saving-mutual-funds-2026), always use direct plans. Since ELSS has a 3-year lock-in:
- You cannot switch from regular to direct during the lock-in period
- Start new ELSS investments in direct plans immediately
- Let existing regular ELSS units complete their lock-in, then redeem and reinvest in direct

## The "Free Advice" Trap

Banks and distributors often present their service as "free" — they don't charge you an advisory fee. But you're paying through:

1. **Higher expense ratio** on regular plans (0.5-1.5% per year)
2. **Biased recommendations** toward high-commission funds (NFOs, thematic funds)
3. **Unnecessary churning** — advisors sometimes recommend switching funds to generate fresh entry commissions
4. **Missing out on better funds** that pay lower commissions

### How to Spot Commission-Driven Advice
- Advisor recommends NFOs (New Fund Offers) frequently
- Suggestions to switch funds every 1-2 years
- Recommendations skew toward small cap and thematic funds (higher commissions)
- No discussion about your goals, risk tolerance, or existing portfolio

## What About Fee-Only Advisors?

SEBI-Registered Investment Advisors (RIAs) charge a flat fee or percentage-based fee and recommend direct plans. This model is transparent:

- **Fee:** Rs 10,000-50,000/year or 0.5-1% of portfolio value
- **Plans:** Direct only (no commission conflict)
- **Value:** Holistic financial planning, tax optimization, goal mapping

For portfolios above Rs 25-50 lakh, an RIA can add significant value through tax-efficient withdrawals, asset allocation, and rebalancing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are direct plan returns guaranteed to be higher?

Yes, because direct and regular plans hold the identical portfolio. The only difference is expense ratio. Direct plan NAV is always higher than regular plan NAV for the same fund.

### Can I have both direct and regular plans of the same fund?

Yes. You'll have separate folios (account numbers) for direct and regular plans. However, there's no reason to hold both — direct is always better if you don't need advisor services.

### Will my bank be upset if I switch to direct plans?

Your relationship manager might discourage you because they lose commission income. Remember, your financial goals matter more than their sales targets.

### Is Kuvera/Groww safe? What if they shut down?

Your mutual fund units are held directly with the AMC and registered with the registrar (CAMS/KFintech), not with the platform. Even if a platform shuts down, your investments are safe. You can always access them through MF Central or the AMC directly.

### How do I track my direct plan investments?

Use MF Central (official), CAS statements (emailed monthly by CAMS/KFintech), or platforms like Kuvera that provide portfolio analytics.
