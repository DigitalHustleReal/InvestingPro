# Best Credit Cards in India 2026 — Expert Picks

Choosing the right credit card can save you lakhs over a lifetime. With dozens of issuers and hundreds of cards available in India, finding the perfect fit requires understanding your spending patterns, lifestyle, and financial goals.

## Key Takeaways

- The best credit card depends on your primary spending category — travel, shopping, fuel, or general rewards
- Premium cards with high annual fees can deliver outsized value if you spend enough to unlock benefits
- Entry-level cards with no annual fee are ideal for building credit history
- Always compare reward rates, annual fees, and hidden charges before applying
- Your CIBIL score (750+) significantly impacts approval chances and credit limits

## Top Credit Cards in India for 2026

### 1. HDFC Infinia — Best Overall Premium Card

The HDFC Infinia remains the gold standard for premium credit cards in India. With uncapped reward points, complimentary airport lounge access worldwide, and a concierge service, it delivers exceptional value for high spenders.

- **Annual Fee:** ₹12,500 (waived on annual spend of ₹10 lakh+)
- **Reward Rate:** 3.3% on all spends (5 reward points per ₹150)
- **Lounge Access:** Unlimited domestic and international
- **Best For:** High spenders with annual spend above ₹8-10 lakh

### 2. SBI SimplyCLICK — Best for Online Shopping

SBI's SimplyCLICK card offers accelerated rewards on online purchases from Amazon, BookMyShow, Cleartrip, and other partners.

- **Annual Fee:** ₹499 (reversed on ₹1 lakh annual spend)
- **Reward Rate:** Up to 2.5% on partner platforms, 1.25% on other online spends
- **Welcome Benefit:** ₹500 Amazon voucher
- **Best For:** Online shoppers spending ₹5,000-15,000 monthly online

### 3. HDFC Regalia — Best for Travel

The HDFC Regalia offers strong travel benefits including lounge access, milestone rewards, and flexible point redemption against travel bookings.

- **Annual Fee:** ₹2,500 (waived on ₹3 lakh annual spend)
- **Reward Rate:** Up to 4 reward points per ₹150
- **Lounge Access:** 12 complimentary visits per year (domestic + international)
- **Best For:** Frequent travellers spending ₹2.5-5 lakh annually

### 4. Amazon Pay ICICI — Best No Annual Fee Card

This co-branded card offers the best value proposition for Amazon shoppers with zero annual fee and strong cashback rates.

- **Annual Fee:** Nil (lifetime free)
- **Reward Rate:** Up to 5% on Amazon (Prime members), 2% on partner platforms, 1% elsewhere
- **Best For:** Amazon shoppers and those wanting zero-cost credit history building

### 5. Axis Bank Flipkart — Best for Flipkart Users

- **Annual Fee:** ₹500 (waived on ₹2 lakh annual spend)
- **Reward Rate:** 5% cashback on Flipkart and Myntra, 4% on preferred partners
- **Best For:** Regular Flipkart and Myntra shoppers

### 6. HDFC MoneyBack+ — Best Entry-Level Rewards Card

- **Annual Fee:** ₹500 (waived on ₹50,000 annual spend)
- **Reward Rate:** 2 reward points per ₹150 on all spends
- **Welcome Benefit:** 500 reward points
- **Best For:** First-time credit card users building credit

### 7. IndianOil Citi — Best for Fuel

- **Annual Fee:** ₹1,000 (waived on spend criteria)
- **Reward Rate:** Up to 6.3% savings on fuel at IndianOil stations
- **Fuel Surcharge Waiver:** 1% surcharge waiver across all fuel stations
- **Best For:** Daily commuters spending ₹3,000-8,000 monthly on fuel

## How to Choose the Right Credit Card

### Step 1: Identify Your Spending Pattern

Track your monthly expenses for 2-3 months. Where does most of your money go — online shopping, groceries, dining, fuel, or travel? Choose a card that rewards your highest spending category.

### Step 2: Check Your CIBIL Score

Your CIBIL score determines which cards you can access. Premium cards typically require 750+ scores. Check your score for free before applying. Learn [how to improve your CIBIL score](/articles/how-to-improve-cibil-score-fast) if needed.

### Step 3: Calculate Net Value

Annual benefits minus annual fee equals net value. A card with ₹2,500 annual fee that gives you ₹10,000 in rewards is better than a free card giving ₹3,000 in rewards.

### Step 4: Read the Fine Print

Check for fuel surcharge waivers, foreign transaction charges, interest-free period, late payment fees, and reward point expiry policies.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which credit card gives the highest rewards in India?

The HDFC Infinia offers one of the highest reward rates at approximately 3.3% on all spends. However, the "best" rewards depend on your spending pattern. Category-specific cards like Amazon Pay ICICI (5% on Amazon) can deliver higher returns in specific categories.

### What CIBIL score do I need for a credit card?

Most credit cards require a CIBIL score of 700+. Premium cards like HDFC Infinia or Regalia typically require 750+. Entry-level secured cards may accept lower scores. You can check your CIBIL score for free on the CIBIL website.

### Is it worth paying an annual fee for a credit card?

Yes, if your annual spending is high enough to earn rewards exceeding the fee. For example, if a card costs ₹2,500 annually but you earn ₹8,000 in rewards, the net benefit is ₹5,500. Calculate this before choosing between free and paid cards.

### How many credit cards should I have?

There is no fixed number, but 2-3 cards covering different spending categories (travel, shopping, fuel) can maximize rewards. Having multiple cards can also improve your credit utilization ratio, which benefits your CIBIL score.

### Can I get a credit card without income proof?

Some banks offer credit cards against fixed deposits (secured credit cards) without traditional income proof. These are useful for students, freelancers, and those building credit history for the first time.
