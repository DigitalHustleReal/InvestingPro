# HDFC Regalia vs HDFC Infinia — Which Premium Card?

HDFC Bank dominates India's premium credit card market with two flagship offerings: the Regalia and the Infinia. Both offer excellent rewards, airport lounge access, and travel benefits — but they target very different spenders. This head-to-head comparison will help you decide which one matches your spending profile.

## Key Takeaways

- HDFC Infinia is India's top-tier card with unlimited lounge access and uncapped rewards — but requires ₹10+ lakh annual spend
- HDFC Regalia offers 80% of the Infinia's benefits at a fraction of the cost and qualification requirement
- For annual spends under ₹5 lakh, the Regalia delivers better value per rupee spent
- For annual spends above ₹8-10 lakh, the Infinia's uncapped rewards and unlimited lounges justify the premium
- Both cards are invitation-only or require relationship with HDFC Bank

## Deep Dive: Where Each Card Wins

### Reward Points Comparison

**HDFC Regalia:** Earns 4 reward points per ₹150 spent. At a redemption value of approximately ₹0.50 per point, this translates to roughly 1.3% reward rate through the standard catalog — but up to 2.6% when redeemed via HDFC SmartBuy for flights and hotels.

**HDFC Infinia:** Earns 5 reward points per ₹150 spent. The key difference is that Infinia reward points are valued at approximately ₹1 per point when transferred to airline partners or redeemed via SmartBuy, giving an effective reward rate of 3.3%.

**Winner: Infinia** — The higher earn rate combined with better redemption value makes Infinia reward points worth roughly 2.5x Regalia points.

### Airport Lounge Access

**HDFC Regalia:** 12 complimentary visits per year, split between domestic and international lounges. Once exhausted, each additional visit costs ₹750-1,500.

**HDFC Infinia:** Unlimited complimentary lounge access worldwide through Priority Pass and Visa Infinite. No caps, no per-visit charges, valid for primary and add-on cardholders.

**Winner: Infinia** — If you travel more than 12 times per year, Infinia saves you potentially ₹20,000-40,000 annually in lounge fees.

### Annual Fee and Waiver

**HDFC Regalia:** ₹2,500 annual fee, waived on ₹3 lakh annual spend. Most professionals with a moderate lifestyle can achieve this threshold easily.

**HDFC Infinia:** ₹12,500 annual fee, waived on ₹10 lakh annual spend. This requires roughly ₹83,000 monthly spend, which suits high-income professionals and business owners.

**Winner: Regalia** — Far easier fee waiver threshold, making it effectively free for most target users.

### Travel Insurance

**HDFC Regalia:** Complimentary travel insurance with air accident cover up to ₹50 lakh and other travel benefits.

**HDFC Infinia:** Comprehensive travel insurance with air accident cover up to ₹3 crore, lost baggage cover, flight delay compensation, and emergency medical assistance abroad.

**Winner: Infinia** — The ₹3 crore coverage with comprehensive benefits is significantly superior.

## Which Card Should You Choose?

### Choose HDFC Regalia If:

- Your annual spend is ₹3-8 lakh
- You travel domestically 6-12 times per year
- You want premium benefits without ultra-high income requirements
- You are building towards the Infinia as an upgrade path
- Your annual income is ₹6-15 lakh

### Choose HDFC Infinia If:

- Your annual spend exceeds ₹8-10 lakh
- You travel frequently (12+ times per year, including international)
- You value uncapped rewards and unlimited lounge access
- You want the highest possible travel insurance coverage
- Your annual income exceeds ₹15 lakh (or you have a strong HDFC relationship)

## The Upgrade Path: Regalia to Infinia

Many cardholders start with the Regalia and upgrade to the Infinia as their income and spending grow. HDFC Bank typically considers upgrade requests based on:

1. **Spending pattern:** Consistent high spending on the Regalia (₹8-10 lakh annually)
2. **Relationship value:** Total HDFC banking relationship (savings, FD, loans, investments)
3. **Payment history:** Perfect payment record with no missed EMIs or late fees
4. **Credit score:** 800+ CIBIL score strengthens your case

**Tip:** Maintain a strong HDFC banking relationship across multiple products. Account holders with ₹10+ lakh in total HDFC deposits and investments may receive proactive Infinia invitations.

## Reward Point Redemption Strategies

Both cards earn maximum value when redeemed through **HDFC SmartBuy** for flights and hotels:

- **Flights via SmartBuy:** Points are worth ₹0.50 (Regalia) to ₹1.00 (Infinia) per point
- **Catalog Redemption:** Points are worth significantly less (₹0.20-0.30 per point)
- **Airline Transfer (Infinia only):** Transfer to Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer, InterMiles, or other partners for potentially higher value

**Golden rule:** Never redeem points through the standard catalog if travel redemption is available.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I directly apply for HDFC Infinia?

HDFC Infinia is primarily invitation-only. However, you can request it through your HDFC relationship manager if your income exceeds ₹15 lakh per annum and you have a strong banking relationship. Some customers receive pre-approved offers through HDFC NetBanking.

### Is HDFC Regalia Gold worth considering as a middle option?

The Regalia Gold sits between Regalia and Infinia, offering more lounge visits and slightly better rewards than the Regalia. If you find the Infinia requirement too high but want more than what the Regalia offers, the Regalia Gold is a solid stepping stone.

### Do both cards work internationally?

Yes, both are Visa Infinite cards accepted worldwide. Both charge 2% foreign currency markup. For international travel, the Infinia offers significantly better travel insurance and unlimited international lounge access.

### Can I hold both Regalia and Infinia simultaneously?

Technically possible but not recommended. HDFC typically upgrades your Regalia to Infinia rather than issuing a second card. Holding both does not meaningfully increase benefits and may split your spending, reducing milestone achievement on either card.
