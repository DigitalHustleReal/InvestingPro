# Old vs New Tax Regime Calculator — Which Saves You More in 2026?

Since Budget 2020, Indian taxpayers can choose between the old tax regime (with deductions) and the new tax regime (lower rates, fewer deductions). The new regime was further sweetened in Budget 2023 and has become the default option. But "default" does not mean "better for everyone."

The right choice depends entirely on how many deductions and exemptions you can claim. This guide gives you the tools to decide.

## Key Takeaways

- New regime is default from FY 2023-24 onwards — you must actively opt out to use old regime
- New regime is better if your total deductions are below Rs 3.75-4.25 lakh (depending on income)
- Old regime is better if you have significant deductions: HRA + 80C + 80D + home loan + NPS
- Salaried employees can switch between regimes every year
- Self-employed/business owners who opt for old regime cannot switch back easily

## Tax Slabs Comparison (FY 2025-26)

### New Tax Regime Slabs

| Income Slab | Tax Rate |
|-------------|----------|
| Up to Rs 4,00,000 | Nil |
| Rs 4,00,001 - Rs 8,00,000 | 5% |
| Rs 8,00,001 - Rs 12,00,000 | 10% |
| Rs 12,00,001 - Rs 16,00,000 | 15% |
| Rs 16,00,001 - Rs 20,00,000 | 20% |
| Rs 20,00,001 - Rs 24,00,000 | 25% |
| Above Rs 24,00,000 | 30% |

**Standard deduction:** Rs 75,000
**Section 87A rebate:** Full tax rebate for income up to Rs 12 lakh (effective zero tax up to ~Rs 12.75 lakh after standard deduction)

### Old Tax Regime Slabs

| Income Slab | Tax Rate |
|-------------|----------|
| Up to Rs 2,50,000 | Nil |
| Rs 2,50,001 - Rs 5,00,000 | 5% |
| Rs 5,00,001 - Rs 10,00,000 | 20% |
| Above Rs 10,00,000 | 30% |

**Standard deduction:** Rs 50,000
**Section 87A rebate:** For income up to Rs 5 lakh

## The Breakeven Analysis

The breakeven point is the total deduction amount at which both regimes result in the same tax. If your deductions exceed this, old regime wins; if below, new regime wins.

### Approximate Breakeven Deductions (Beyond Standard Deduction)

| Gross Salary | Breakeven Deduction* | Old Regime Better If |
|-------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Rs 7.5 lakh | ~Rs 1.5 lakh | Deductions > Rs 1.5 lakh |
| Rs 10 lakh | ~Rs 2.5 lakh | Deductions > Rs 2.5 lakh |
| Rs 12 lakh | ~Rs 3.0 lakh | Deductions > Rs 3.0 lakh |
| Rs 15 lakh | ~Rs 3.75 lakh | Deductions > Rs 3.75 lakh |
| Rs 20 lakh | ~Rs 4.25 lakh | Deductions > Rs 4.25 lakh |
| Rs 30 lakh | ~Rs 4.75 lakh | Deductions > Rs 4.75 lakh |
| Rs 50 lakh | ~Rs 5.25 lakh | Deductions > Rs 5.25 lakh |

*Includes all deductions: HRA + 80C + 80D + 24(b) + NPS + others. Excluding standard deduction difference.

## Who Should Choose the New Regime?

The new regime clearly wins for:

1. **Young professionals without HRA claims** — living with parents or in own house
2. **People with minimal investments** — EPF is their only 80C investment
3. **No home loan** — Section 24(b) is the biggest deduction for many
4. **Income up to Rs 12 lakh** — zero tax under new regime (with rebate)
5. **Those who don't want to "invest to save tax"** — new regime doesn't require any tax-saving investments

## Who Should Choose the Old Regime?

The old regime wins for:

1. **Salaried with HRA in metro cities** — HRA alone can be Rs 2-4 lakh deduction
2. **Home loan borrowers** — Rs 2 lakh interest deduction (Section 24(b)) is significant
3. **Parents** — tuition fees for 2 children adds to 80C
4. **Those with health insurance for senior parents** — Rs 50,000 under 80D
5. **NPS investors** — Rs 50,000 additional under 80CCD(1B) (though this is available in new regime for employer NPS)
6. **Multiple deductions stacking** — the more deductions you have, the stronger the old regime case

## How to Switch Between Regimes

### For Salaried Employees
- Choose at the time of filing ITR (not when declaring to employer)
- Can switch every financial year
- Inform your employer at the start of the year for correct TDS calculation
- If you don't inform, employer uses new regime (default)

### For Business/Professional Income
- Once you opt out of the new regime, you cannot switch back (one-time option)
- This restriction makes the choice more consequential
- Consult a CA before deciding

## Impact on Investment Decisions

### If You Choose New Regime
- **Stop investing ONLY for tax saving** — evaluate each investment on merit
- **ELSS is still a good equity fund** even without tax benefit
- **PPF is still tax-free returns** — just no 80C deduction
- **Health insurance is essential** regardless of 80D benefit
- **Skip endowment/ULIP plans** entirely — they were never good investments

### If You Choose Old Regime
- **Maximize 80C:** ELSS SIP + EPF should cover most of it
- **Get Rs 50,000 extra through NPS:** 80CCD(1B) is available only in old regime
- **Ensure adequate health insurance:** 80D deduction for self + family + parents
- **Consider voluntary home loan prepayment:** Section 24(b) and 80C principal deduction

## Common Misconceptions

### "New regime always saves more tax"
False. For anyone with total deductions above Rs 3-4 lakh, the old regime often wins. The higher your income and more deductions you have, the stronger the old regime advantage.

### "I can't change once I choose"
Salaried employees can switch every year. Only business/professional income earners have the one-time restriction.

### "Standard deduction of Rs 75,000 in new regime is a big advantage"
The old regime also has Rs 50,000 standard deduction. The difference is only Rs 25,000 — a tax saving of Rs 5,000-7,500 depending on slab. This alone doesn't justify the new regime.

### "The new regime is simpler, so it must be better"
Simpler does not mean cheaper. If you're leaving Rs 1-2.5 lakh in tax savings on the table for "simplicity," that's an expensive choice.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I have a mix of old and new regime in the same year?

No. You must choose one regime for the entire financial year. All income sources are taxed under the chosen regime.

### Does my employer need to know which regime I'll choose?

Your employer uses the default (new regime) for TDS unless you inform them otherwise. You can declare your intended regime to HR at the start of the year.

### What about capital gains tax? Does the regime affect it?

No. Capital gains tax rates (LTCG, STCG) are the same under both regimes. The regime choice only affects the taxation of income from salary, house property, and other sources.

### I'm confused — should I just pick the default new regime?

If your deductions (HRA + 80C + 80D + home loan interest + NPS) exceed Rs 3.5 lakh, you should at least calculate both scenarios before defaulting. Use our tax calculator or consult a CA.

### Can freelancers choose the old regime?

Yes, freelancers filing under "income from business/profession" can opt out of the new regime. But the one-time switching restriction applies — once you go back to old, you generally can't return to new.
