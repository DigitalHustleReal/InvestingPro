# Best Fuel Credit Cards in India — Save on Petrol

With petrol prices hovering around ₹100+ per litre in most Indian cities, fuel is one of the biggest monthly expenses for commuters. The right fuel credit card can save you ₹500-2,000 per month through surcharge waivers, accelerated rewards at fuel stations, and cashback on fuel purchases.

## Key Takeaways

- Credit card fuel surcharge is typically 1% of the transaction amount — the right card waives this completely
- Co-branded fuel cards from HPCL, BPCL, and IndianOil offer the highest savings at their respective stations
- Fuel surcharge waiver is capped at ₹100-250 per statement cycle on most cards
- For maximum savings, use co-branded fuel cards at the partner's fuel station and a general rewards card elsewhere
- All fuel transactions below ₹400 and above ₹4,000-5,000 may not qualify for surcharge waiver on some cards

## Understanding Fuel Surcharge

### What Is Fuel Surcharge?

When you swipe your credit card at a petrol pump, the card network (Visa/Mastercard/RuPay) charges a surcharge of approximately 1% of the transaction amount plus GST (18% on the surcharge). On a ₹5,000 fuel transaction, this adds approximately ₹59.

### How Surcharge Waiver Works

Banks waive this surcharge on your statement. The waiver typically applies to:
- Transactions at fuel merchant category code (MCC 5541/5542)
- Transaction amounts between ₹400 and ₹4,000-5,000
- Up to a maximum waiver of ₹100-250 per billing cycle

### Surcharge Waiver vs Fuel Rewards

| Benefit Type | How It Works | Typical Value |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|
| Surcharge Waiver | Reverses the 1% + GST surcharge | ₹50-250/month |
| Fuel Cashback | Additional percentage back on fuel spend | ₹100-500/month |
| Fuel Reward Points | Accelerated points at partner fuel stations | ₹150-600/month |

**Best strategy:** Choose a co-branded fuel card for the accelerated rewards, which stack with the surcharge waiver for maximum total savings.

## How to Maximize Fuel Card Savings

### 1. Match Your Station to Your Card

Use the HDFC-IndianOil card at IndianOil, BPCL-SBI at BPCL, and HPCL-ICICI at HPCL. The accelerated rewards (4-5%) are 3-4x higher than the surcharge waiver alone.

### 2. Keep Transactions in the Sweet Spot

Most surcharge waivers apply to transactions between ₹400 and ₹4,000-5,000. If you need to fill ₹8,000 of fuel, split it into two transactions of ₹4,000 each.

### 3. Avoid Cash Advances at Fuel Stations

Some card machines at fuel stations default to "cash advance" mode. Always ensure the transaction processes as a "purchase" — cash advances carry heavy fees and interest.

### 4. Stack with Fuel Station Loyalty Programs

IndianOil's XTRA Rewards, BPCL's PetroBonus, and HPCL's HP Pay loyalty programs offer additional savings that stack with credit card rewards. Double-dip by using both your fuel card and the station's loyalty program.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which credit card gives the most savings on fuel?

Co-branded fuel cards like the HDFC-IndianOil and BPCL-SBI cards offer the highest total savings (4-5% at partner stations). For non-partner stations, any card with a fuel surcharge waiver saves approximately 1.18% on every transaction.

### Do all credit cards waive fuel surcharge?

No. While many credit cards offer fuel surcharge waiver, it is not universal. Some entry-level and secured cards do not include this benefit. Always check the card's features before applying if fuel savings are a priority.

### Is it better to pay for fuel with a credit card or UPI?

Credit cards offer surcharge waiver and rewards (total 2-5% savings), while UPI offers no rewards at most fuel stations. For fuel specifically, credit cards with fuel benefits are almost always better than UPI or debit cards.

### Can I use any credit card at any fuel station?

Yes, any Visa/Mastercard/RuPay credit card works at any fuel station. However, co-branded fuel cards earn accelerated rewards only at their partner's stations. The basic surcharge waiver applies at all fuel stations.
