# Personal Loan vs Credit Card EMI — Which Costs Less?

You need Rs 2 lakh for a medical expense, home renovation, or a big purchase. Two common options: take a personal loan from a bank, or convert the amount to EMI on your credit card. Both spread the cost over monthly instalments — but the true cost can differ dramatically depending on the fine print.

## Key Takeaways

- Personal loan interest: 10.50-24% per annum (depends on profile)
- Credit card EMI conversion: 12-18% per annum (often disguised as "processing fee")
- For amounts above Rs 2 lakh and tenures over 12 months, personal loans usually win
- Credit card EMI is faster (instant approval) and better for smaller amounts
- Always calculate the total cost (interest + processing + GST) — not just the monthly EMI

## Understanding the Two Options

### Personal Loan
A separate loan from a bank or NBFC, typically Rs 50,000 to Rs 40 lakh, with fixed EMIs over 12-60 months. It's disbursed to your bank account and is independent of your credit card.

**Key features:**
- Interest rate: 10.50-24% per annum
- Processing fee: 1-3% of loan amount
- Tenure: 12 to 60 months
- No collateral required
- Separate credit assessment

### Credit Card EMI Conversion
Your credit card issuer lets you convert large transactions (or outstanding balance) into fixed EMIs. The amount remains on your credit card limit and is repaid over 3-24 months.

**Key features:**
- Interest rate: 12-18% per annum (or flat rate of 1-1.5% per month)
- Processing fee: Rs 199-999 per conversion
- Tenure: 3 to 24 months (rarely longer)
- Uses your existing credit limit
- Instant approval (pre-approved for most cardholders)

## The True Cost Comparison

Here's where most people get confused. Credit card EMI schemes often quote a "flat" interest rate that looks lower but is actually more expensive than a "reducing balance" personal loan rate.

### Flat Rate vs Reducing Balance Rate

- **Flat rate:** Interest is calculated on the original loan amount for the entire tenure
- **Reducing balance rate:** Interest is calculated on the outstanding principal, which decreases each month

**A flat rate of 1.2% per month (14.4% annually) is equivalent to approximately 24-26% on a reducing balance basis.**

### Example: Rs 2 Lakh Borrowed for 12 Months

**Option A: Personal Loan at 12% (reducing balance)**
- EMI: Rs 17,769
- Total interest paid: Rs 13,228
- Processing fee (2%): Rs 4,000
- **Total cost: Rs 17,228**

**Option B: Credit Card EMI at 14% flat rate**
- EMI: Rs 19,000
- Total interest paid: Rs 28,000
- Processing fee: Rs 499
- **Total cost: Rs 28,499**

**Option C: Credit Card EMI at "zero cost" with processing fee**
- EMI: Rs 16,667 (no interest)
- Processing fee: Rs 4,999
- GST on fee: Rs 900
- **Total cost: Rs 5,899**

In this case, the "zero-cost" EMI is cheapest — but check whether it's truly zero or has hidden charges.

## When to Choose Personal Loan

### 1. Large Amounts (Above Rs 2-3 Lakh)
Credit card EMI is limited by your available credit limit. Personal loans can go up to Rs 40 lakh for salaried professionals with good income.

### 2. Longer Tenure (12+ Months)
Credit card EMI typically maxes out at 24 months. Personal loans extend to 60 months, reducing your monthly burden.

### 3. Lower Interest Rate
If your credit score is 750+ and you have stable employment, personal loan rates of 10.50-13% (reducing balance) are often cheaper than credit card EMI rates.

### 4. Debt Consolidation
If you have multiple credit card dues, a personal loan at 12% to pay off credit card revolving debt at 36-42% saves significant money.

### 5. No Impact on Credit Utilisation
Credit card EMI blocks your credit limit, increasing your credit utilisation ratio (which can lower your CIBIL score). A personal loan is a separate credit line.

## When to Choose Credit Card EMI

### 1. Small Amounts (Under Rs 1-2 Lakh)
For a Rs 50,000 purchase, the personal loan processing fee (Rs 1,000-1,500) and effort aren't worth it. Credit card EMI is simpler.

### 2. Zero-Cost or Low-Cost EMI on Purchases
Many merchants (Amazon, Flipkart, Croma, Apple) offer genuine zero-cost EMI on [select credit cards](/credit-cards). The merchant or bank absorbs the interest cost. Always verify there's no hidden processing fee.

### 3. Instant Approval Needed
Credit card EMI conversion takes minutes — you can do it through the bank app. Personal loan disbursement takes 24 hours to a week.

### 4. Short Tenure (3-6 Months)
For amounts you'll pay back in 3-6 months, credit card EMI is convenient and the interest differential is small.

### 5. Promotional Offers
Banks occasionally offer credit card EMI at 0% or reduced rates to specific cardholders. If you get such an offer, it's usually the cheapest option.

## Hidden Costs to Watch Out For

### Credit Card EMI Hidden Charges
1. **GST on interest:** 18% GST is charged on interest/processing fees
2. **Foreclosure penalty:** 2-3% if you want to close the EMI early
3. **Reduced credit limit:** Your available limit drops by the EMI amount
4. **Late payment charges:** Miss one EMI and the entire conversion may revert to revolving credit at 36-42%
5. **Bounce charges:** Rs 500-1,000 if the auto-debit fails

### Personal Loan Hidden Charges
1. **Processing fee:** 1-3% of loan amount (negotiate this — many banks waive it for existing customers)
2. **Prepayment penalty:** Some banks charge 2-5% on prepaid amount (check before signing)
3. **Insurance:** Banks aggressively push loan protection insurance; it's usually overpriced and optional
4. **Documentation charges:** Some banks add Rs 500-2,000 for paperwork
5. **GST:** 18% on processing fee and all charges

## The Smart Approach

### For Planned Large Purchases
1. Check if your credit card offers genuine zero-cost EMI at the merchant
2. If not, compare personal loan rates from 3-4 banks using our [loan comparison tools](/loans)
3. Calculate total cost including all fees and GST
4. Choose the option with the lowest total cost, not lowest EMI

### For Emergency Expenses
1. Use your emergency fund first (you should have 6 months of expenses)
2. If needed, check credit card EMI conversion (fastest)
3. Apply for a personal loan if the amount is large or you need more than 12 months
4. Avoid credit card revolving credit at all costs — 36-42% interest rate is devastating

### For Debt Consolidation
1. If you have outstanding credit card dues at 36-42%, get a personal loan at 12-15% to pay them off
2. Close the credit card dues immediately
3. Set up auto-debit for the personal loan EMI
4. Don't accumulate new credit card debt

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does credit card EMI affect CIBIL score?

Yes. The EMI amount blocks your credit limit, increasing your credit utilisation ratio. High utilisation (above 30%) can lower your CIBIL score. Read our [CIBIL score guide](/articles/how-to-improve-cibil-score-fast) for optimization tips.

### Can I foreclose a credit card EMI?

Yes, most banks allow foreclosure through the app or customer care. A 2-3% foreclosure charge may apply. Check the terms before converting.

### Which banks offer the best personal loan rates?

For salaried professionals with 750+ CIBIL score: SBI, HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, and Kotak Mahindra Bank typically offer rates between 10.50-12.50%. Compare current rates on our [loans page](/loans).

### Is "no-cost EMI" really free?

Often, the MRP is inflated to include the interest cost, or there's a processing fee that represents the interest. Check the product price for one-time payment vs EMI price — if they're the same and there's no processing fee, it's genuinely free.

### Can I get a personal loan if I already have home and car loans?

Yes, but your FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio) must be within limits. Banks typically allow total EMIs to be 50-60% of gross income.
