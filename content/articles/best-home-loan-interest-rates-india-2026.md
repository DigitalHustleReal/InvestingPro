## How Home Loan Rates Are Determined

Since October 2019, RBI mandates that all new floating-rate home loans be linked to an external benchmark. Most banks use the RBI repo rate.

### The Formula
**Your home loan rate = External Benchmark Rate + Spread (Bank's Margin)**

Example:
- RBI Repo Rate: 6.25%
- Bank's Spread: 2.00%
- **Your rate: 8.25%**

The spread varies based on:
1. **Your CIBIL score:** 800+ gets the lowest spread; below 700 gets a higher spread
2. **Loan amount:** Larger loans sometimes get better rates
3. **LTV ratio:** Lower down payment (higher LTV) means higher spread
4. **Employment type:** Government employees and salaried professionals get better rates than self-employed
5. **Property type:** Ready-to-move properties get better rates than under-construction

### When Rates Change
External benchmark rates must be reset at least once every quarter. When RBI cuts the repo rate, your home loan EMI should decrease within 3 months.

## How to Get the Lowest Home Loan Rate

### 1. Build a Strong CIBIL Score (750+)
Your credit score is the single biggest factor. Banks offer their best rates to 750+ borrowers, with premium rates for 800+.

- Pay all credit card bills in full before the due date
- Keep credit utilisation below 30%
- Don't apply for multiple loans simultaneously
- Check your score 6 months before applying and fix any errors

Read our detailed [CIBIL score improvement guide](/articles/how-to-improve-cibil-score-fast).

### 2. Compare Across 5+ Lenders
Don't just walk into your salary bank. Apply to at least 5 lenders (2-3 banks + 1-2 NBFCs). Rate differences of 0.25-0.50% are common between lenders for the same profile.

### 3. Negotiate the Processing Fee
Processing fees are negotiable. Many banks waive or reduce the processing fee for:
- Existing customers (salary account holders)
- High-value loans (above Rs 50 lakh)
- Festive season offers (Diwali, Republic Day)
- Government employees

### 4. Consider Women Applicants
Most banks offer 0.05% lower interest rates for women borrowers. If your spouse is a co-applicant and a woman, make her the primary applicant to avail this benefit.

### 5. Opt for Lower LTV
If you can afford a higher down payment (25-30% instead of minimum 10-20%), banks often offer a lower spread on your interest rate.

### 6. Choose the Right Benchmark
Repo-linked (EBLR) loans transmit rate cuts faster than MCLR-linked loans. Ask for an EBLR-linked loan.

### 7. Check for Special Schemes
Banks periodically offer promotional rates:
- **SBI "Festive Home Loan"** — reduced processing fee and rates
- **HDFC "Home Loan Mela"** — discounted processing fees
- **Government employee schemes** — additional rate discounts
- **Pre-approved offers** — existing customers get lower rates

## Fixed vs Floating Rate Home Loans

### Floating Rate (Recommended)
- Interest rate moves with the benchmark (up or down)
- Most common in India (95%+ of home loans)
- Benefits from rate cuts automatically
- Zero prepayment penalty (RBI mandate)

### Fixed Rate
- Rate remains constant for a period (usually 2-5 years, rarely the full tenure)
- Typically 0.50-1.00% higher than floating at the time of booking
- Prepayment penalty may apply (2-3%)
- Suitable if you expect rates to rise significantly

### Our Recommendation
In the current environment with the RBI in a rate-cutting cycle, floating rate loans are preferred. You benefit from future rate cuts, and you have the flexibility to prepay without penalty.

## Balance Transfer: Switch to a Lower Rate

If you're currently paying a higher rate on your existing home loan, a balance transfer can save lakhs. See our detailed guide on [how to transfer your home loan](/articles/how-to-transfer-home-loan-lower-interest-rate) for the step-by-step process.

**Quick check — is a balance transfer worth it?**
- Your current rate is 0.25%+ higher than available rates: Worth exploring
- Your current rate is 0.50%+ higher: Strongly recommended
- Remaining tenure is less than 5 years: Usually not worth the effort
- Remaining tenure is 10+ years: Even a 0.25% reduction saves significantly

## Special Segments

### Home Loans for Self-Employed
Self-employed borrowers typically get rates 0.15-0.30% higher than salaried. Key requirements:
- 3+ years of business vintage
- Last 3 years' ITR and financial statements
- GST returns (if applicable)
- Business proof (registration, current account)

### Home Loans for NRIs
NRIs can get home loans in India with these considerations:
- Rates are 0.10-0.25% higher than resident rates
- Maximum tenure: Usually 15-20 years (vs 30 for residents)
- NRE/NRO account required for EMI payments
- Additional documentation: passport, visa, overseas employment proof

### Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY)
If eligible, PMAY provides interest subsidy of:
- **EWS (up to Rs 3 lakh income):** 6.5% subsidy on Rs 6 lakh for 20 years
- **LIG (Rs 3-6 lakh):** 6.5% subsidy on Rs 6 lakh for 20 years
- **MIG-I (Rs 6-12 lakh):** 4% subsidy on Rs 9 lakh for 20 years
- **MIG-II (Rs 12-18 lakh):** 3% subsidy on Rs 12 lakh for 20 years

Check eligibility on the PMAY portal before applying.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which bank has the lowest home loan rate in India right now?

As of April 2026, SBI and Bank of Baroda offer the lowest starting rates at 8.20% for prime borrowers (CIBIL 800+, salaried, low LTV). Your actual rate depends on your profile.

### How often does the home loan interest rate change?

For EBLR-linked loans, the benchmark resets quarterly. Your rate changes whenever the RBI changes the repo rate and your bank adjusts the benchmark.

### Can I switch from MCLR to EBLR with the same bank?

Yes, most banks allow this with a small conversion fee (Rs 5,000-10,000). If your MCLR rate hasn't reduced despite repo rate cuts, this switch is advisable.

### Does a pre-approved home loan guarantee the advertised rate?

Not always. The pre-approved rate may change based on the property value, LTV ratio, and final credit assessment. Treat pre-approved offers as indicative.

### How long does home loan approval take?

Typically 7-15 working days from application to disbursement. Pre-approved customers may get faster processing (3-7 days). Documentation completeness is the biggest factor in speed.
