# GST Registration Guide for Small Business in India — Who Needs It, How to Apply, Costs

If you run a small business in India — whether it is a chai shop, a freelance design studio, or an online store on Amazon — you have probably heard about GST. Maybe your CA told you to register. Maybe a client asked for a GST invoice. Maybe you are just confused about whether you even need it.

This guide breaks down everything about GST registration for small businesses in simple, practical language. No jargon overload. Just the stuff you actually need to know.

## Key Takeaways

- GST registration is mandatory if your annual turnover exceeds **₹40 lakh** (goods) or **₹20 lakh** (services) — lower thresholds apply in special category states
- **Interstate sellers** and **e-commerce sellers** must register regardless of turnover
- The **Composition Scheme** lets you pay lower tax (1-6%) with simpler compliance if turnover is under ₹1.5 crore
- Registration is **free** on the GST portal — no government fee
- You need PAN, Aadhaar, bank account proof, and business address proof to register
- Late filing attracts a penalty of **₹50/day** (₹20/day for nil returns) plus 18% annual interest on unpaid tax

## What Is GST and Why Should Small Businesses Care?

GST (Goods and Services Tax) replaced a messy web of indirect taxes — VAT, service tax, excise duty, entertainment tax — with one unified tax in July 2017. Before GST, a small manufacturer had to deal with excise at the factory gate, VAT at the state level, and CST if selling across state borders. It was a compliance nightmare.

Now there is one tax, one return system, and one registration.

**Why it matters for your small business:**

1. **Legal compliance** — Operating without GST when required can attract penalties up to 100% of the tax due, or even prosecution for evasion
2. **Input Tax Credit (ITC)** — You can claim credit for GST paid on purchases and reduce your tax liability. A furniture maker who buys wood worth ₹1 lakh (paying ₹18,000 GST) can deduct that ₹18,000 from the GST collected on sales
3. **Business credibility** — Many B2B clients and government tenders require a GSTIN. No GSTIN, no deal
4. **E-commerce access** — Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho, and other marketplaces require GST registration to sell on their platforms
5. **Interstate trade** — You cannot legally sell goods or services across state borders without GST registration

## Who Must Register for GST? (Mandatory Registration)

Not every business needs GST registration. Here is who **must** register:

### 1. Turnover-Based Threshold

| Business Type | Threshold (Regular States) | Threshold (Special Category States*) |
|---|---|---|
| Selling goods | ₹40 lakh | ₹20 lakh |
| Providing services | ₹20 lakh | ₹10 lakh |
| Both goods + services | ₹20 lakh (service threshold applies) | ₹10 lakh |

*Special category states: Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim, Tripura, Uttarakhand, Himachal Pradesh, Jammu & Kashmir, Ladakh

**Important:** The threshold is based on **aggregate turnover** — which includes all taxable supplies, exempt supplies, and exports. It is your total business turnover, not just taxable turnover.

### 2. Mandatory Registration Regardless of Turnover

Even if your turnover is ₹5 lakh, you **must** register if:

- **Interstate supply** — You sell goods or services from one state to another (a Delhi designer serving a Mumbai client)
- **E-commerce sellers** — You sell on Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho, or any e-commerce platform
- **E-commerce operators** — You run a marketplace platform
- **Casual taxable person** — You temporarily supply goods/services in a state where you have no fixed place of business (a Jaipur caterer doing an event in Goa)
- **Reverse charge liability** — You receive supplies on which you must pay GST instead of the supplier
- **Non-resident taxable person** — Foreign businesses supplying in India
- **Input Service Distributor (ISD)** — Companies distributing ITC across branches
- **TDS/TCS deductors** — Government bodies or e-commerce operators deducting tax at source
- **Agents** supplying on behalf of another person

### Real-World Examples

**Ravi's Chai Shop (Lucknow):**
Annual revenue: ₹18 lakh. Sells only in Lucknow. Since he sells goods in a regular state and turnover is below ₹40 lakh — **no GST registration needed**. But he can register voluntarily if he wants ITC benefits on his supplies (tea, sugar, milk, gas, equipment).

**Priya's Freelance Graphic Design:**
Annual billing: ₹22 lakh. All clients are in India, some in other states. Since she provides services and her turnover exceeds ₹20 lakh — **GST registration is mandatory**. Plus, if she serves clients in other states, interstate supply rules make registration mandatory regardless of turnover.

**Amit's Online T-Shirt Store (Flipkart):**
Annual sales: ₹8 lakh. Sells only on Flipkart. Even though his turnover is way below ₹40 lakh — **GST registration is mandatory** because he sells on an e-commerce platform.

## Types of GST Registration

### 1. Regular Registration (Most Businesses)

This is the standard registration where you:
- Charge GST at applicable rates (5%, 12%, 18%, or 28%) on your invoices
- File monthly or quarterly returns (depending on turnover)
- Can claim Input Tax Credit on business purchases
- Can sell interstate without restrictions

**Best for:** Businesses with B2B clients, those who need ITC, and those selling across state borders.

### 2. Composition Scheme Registration

A simplified scheme for small businesses who want minimal compliance headaches.

**Eligibility:**
- Annual turnover up to **₹1.5 crore** (goods)
- Annual turnover up to **₹50 lakh** (services — introduced later)
- Must not sell interstate
- Must not supply through e-commerce platforms
- Must not be a manufacturer of certain goods (ice cream, tobacco, pan masala)

**Tax rates under Composition Scheme:**

| Business Type | Composition Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Manufacturers | 1% (0.5% CGST + 0.5% SGST) |
| Traders (goods) | 1% (0.5% CGST + 0.5% SGST) |
| Restaurants (non-AC, no liquor license) | 5% (2.5% CGST + 2.5% SGST) |
| Service providers (and mixed suppliers) | 6% (3% CGST + 3% SGST) |

**Restrictions:**
- Cannot collect GST from customers (must absorb the tax)
- Cannot claim Input Tax Credit
- Cannot issue a tax invoice (only a "Bill of Supply")
- Cannot sell outside your state
- Must mention "Composition Taxable Person" on every notice and bill

### 3. Voluntary Registration

Even if you are below the threshold, you can register voluntarily. This makes sense if:
- You want to claim ITC on business purchases (useful if you buy expensive equipment)
- Your clients need GST invoices
- You plan to scale above the threshold soon
- You want to build business credibility

**Note:** Once you register voluntarily, you must comply with all GST return filing requirements. You cannot just sit on the registration and do nothing.

## Composition Scheme vs Regular GST — Detailed Comparison

This is one of the most common questions small business owners ask. Here is a clear comparison:

| Feature | Regular GST | Composition Scheme |
|---|---|---|
| **Turnover limit** | No limit | ₹1.5 crore (goods), ₹50 lakh (services) |
| **Tax rate** | 5% / 12% / 18% / 28% (depending on goods/services) | 1% to 6% (flat, depending on business type) |
| **Input Tax Credit** | Yes — claim GST paid on purchases | No — cannot claim ITC |
| **Interstate sales** | Allowed | Not allowed |
| **E-commerce sales** | Allowed | Not allowed |
| **Invoice type** | Tax Invoice (with GST breakup) | Bill of Supply (no GST breakup) |
| **Return frequency** | Monthly/Quarterly (GSTR-1, GSTR-3B) | Quarterly (CMP-08) + Annual (GSTR-4) |
| **Compliance effort** | Higher — detailed invoices, ITC matching | Lower — simpler returns |
| **Customer impact** | Customers get ITC on your invoices | Customers cannot claim ITC |

### When to Choose What?

**Choose Composition Scheme if:**
- You sell directly to end consumers (retail shop, restaurant, local service)
- Your turnover is under ₹1.5 crore
- You do not sell outside your state
- You want simple, low-cost compliance
- Your customers do not need GST invoices (B2C businesses)

**Choose Regular GST if:**
- You sell to other businesses (B2B) — your customers need ITC
- You sell interstate or export
- You sell on e-commerce platforms
- You want to claim ITC on your purchases (especially if you buy expensive raw materials or equipment)
- Your margins are thin and the tax burden from not claiming ITC would hurt

**Real Example — Suresh's Stationery Shop (Pune):**
Suresh buys stationery worth ₹30 lakh/year (paying ₹5.4 lakh GST at 18%) and sells for ₹48 lakh/year.

- **Under Composition:** He pays 1% on ₹48 lakh = ₹48,000. But he cannot claim the ₹5.4 lakh ITC. Total tax cost = ₹48,000.
- **Under Regular:** He collects 18% GST on sales = ₹8.64 lakh. He claims ₹5.4 lakh ITC. Net tax payable = ₹3.24 lakh.

Wait — Composition looks cheaper? Yes, in this case it is. But if Suresh's clients are businesses who want GST invoices to claim their own ITC, they will switch to a competitor who gives tax invoices. The choice is not just about your tax — it is about your customer base.

## Documents Needed for GST Registration

Gather these before starting the application:

### For Sole Proprietorship

| Document | Details |
|---|---|
| PAN card | Of the proprietor |
| Aadhaar card | Of the proprietor |
| Photograph | Passport-size, recent |
| Bank account proof | Cancelled cheque, passbook first page, or bank statement |
| Address proof of business | Electricity bill, rent agreement, property tax receipt, or NOC from owner |
| If rented premises | Rent agreement + NOC from landlord + landlord's address proof |

### For Partnership Firm / LLP

- Partnership deed / LLP agreement
- PAN of the firm
- PAN and Aadhaar of all partners
- Address proof of business
- Bank account of the firm
- Authorization letter nominating the authorized signatory

### For Private Limited Company

- Certificate of Incorporation
- PAN of the company
- PAN and Aadhaar of all directors
- MOA and AOA
- Board resolution appointing authorized signatory
- Address proof of registered office
- Bank account of the company

**Pro Tip:** All documents must be uploaded in **JPEG or PDF format**, with file size under **1 MB each**. Scan them in advance to save time during the application.

## Step-by-Step GST Registration Process (Online)

Registration is done entirely online on the **GST portal (www.gst.gov.in)**. It is free — there is no government fee.

### Step 1: Visit the GST Portal

Go to **www.gst.gov.in** and click on **"Services" > "Registration" > "New Registration"**.

### Step 2: Fill Part A — Basic Details

- Select **"Taxpayer"** as the type of registration
- Select your **state** and **district**
- Enter your **legal name** (as on PAN card)
- Enter your **PAN number**
- Enter your **email address** and **mobile number**
- An OTP will be sent to both your email and mobile — enter both OTPs to verify
- You will receive a **Temporary Reference Number (TRN)**

### Step 3: Log In with TRN

- Go back to the GST portal
- Click **"Services" > "Registration" > "New Registration"**
- Select **"Temporary Reference Number (TRN)"**
- Enter your TRN and verify with OTP

### Step 4: Fill Part B — Detailed Application (Form GST REG-01)

This has 10 sections:

1. **Business Details** — Trade name, constitution (proprietorship/partnership/company), date of commencement
2. **Promoter/Partner Details** — Name, PAN, Aadhaar, photo, designation
3. **Authorized Signatory** — Who will sign GST returns and documents
4. **Authorized Representative** — Optional (your CA or tax consultant)
5. **Principal Place of Business** — Address, nature (own/rented/leased), proof
6. **Additional Places of Business** — Branches, warehouses, godowns
7. **Goods and Services** — Select HSN codes (for goods) or SAC codes (for services) that apply to your business. You can add up to 5 major categories
8. **Bank Accounts** — Details of your business bank account(s)
9. **State-Specific Information** — Varies by state
10. **Verification** — Digital signature, e-sign, or EVC verification

### Step 5: Upload Documents

Upload all required documents as listed above. Make sure:
- File size is under 1 MB each
- Format is JPEG or PDF
- Documents are clear and readable

### Step 6: Submit and Verify

- Submit the application using **DSC (Digital Signature Certificate)** — mandatory for companies and LLPs
- For proprietors and partnerships, you can use **Aadhaar-based e-Sign** or **EVC (Electronic Verification Code)**
- You will get an **Application Reference Number (ARN)**

### Step 7: Application Processing

- The GST officer reviews your application within **7 working days**
- If everything is in order, GSTIN is issued
- If clarification is needed, you receive a notice in **Form GST REG-03** — respond within 7 days using **Form GST REG-04**
- If deficiencies are found, you may receive a rejection notice in **Form GST REG-05**

### Step 8: GSTIN Issued

- Once approved, you receive your **15-digit GSTIN** (Goods and Services Tax Identification Number)
- The registration certificate is available for download on the GST portal
- Your GSTIN is in the format: **22AAAAA0000A1Z5** (first 2 digits = state code, next 10 = PAN)

**Timeline:** The entire process typically takes **3-7 working days** if documents are in order. Aadhaar-verified applications can get approved in as little as 3 days.

## GST Rates — What Rate Applies to Your Business?

GST has four main rate slabs:

| GST Rate | Common Items / Services |
|---|---|
| **0% (Exempt)** | Fresh fruits, vegetables, milk, eggs, curd, bread, salt, books, education, healthcare |
| **5%** | Packaged food, tea, coffee, spices, apparel under ₹1,000, footwear under ₹1,000, economy class flights, cab rides |
| **12%** | Processed food, ghee, fruit juices, mobile phones, sewing machines, business class flights |
| **18%** | Most goods and services — restaurant meals (AC restaurants), IT services, financial services, telecom, branded garments, cement, steel |
| **28%** | Luxury and sin items — cars, ACs, washing machines, TVs, aerated drinks, tobacco, pan masala, gambling |

### GST for Common Small Businesses

| Business Type | Likely GST Rate |
|---|---|
| Local restaurant (non-AC) | 5% (without ITC) |
| AC restaurant | 5% (without ITC) |
| Freelance designer/developer | 18% |
| Online seller (goods) | Depends on product — 5% to 28% |
| Coaching institute | 18% |
| Beauty salon/spa | 18% |
| Sweet shop | 5% (sweets) |
| Clothing boutique | 5% (under ₹1,000) or 12% (above ₹1,000) |
| Electrician/plumber services | 18% |
| Gym/fitness center | 18% |

**Note:** Restaurant GST was simplified to a flat 5% without ITC for all restaurants (AC and non-AC). Only restaurants inside 5-star hotels charge 18%.

## Input Tax Credit (ITC) — Explained Simply

ITC is the biggest advantage of regular GST registration. Think of it as a refund mechanism.

### How ITC Works

You buy raw materials for ₹1,00,000 + ₹18,000 GST = ₹1,18,000 total.
You sell finished goods for ₹2,00,000 + ₹36,000 GST = ₹2,36,000 total.

Without ITC, you pay ₹36,000 to the government.
With ITC, you pay only ₹36,000 - ₹18,000 = **₹18,000** to the government.

The ₹18,000 GST you paid on purchases is your Input Tax Credit. You are essentially paying GST only on the **value you added** — not on the entire sale price.

### Conditions to Claim ITC

1. You must have a **tax invoice** (not a bill of supply) from your supplier
2. The supplier must have **actually paid** that GST to the government
3. You must have **received** the goods or services
4. You must file your **returns on time** (GSTR-3B)
5. The goods/services must be used for **business purposes** (not personal use)
6. Your supplier's invoice must reflect in your **GSTR-2B** (auto-generated from supplier's GSTR-1)

### ITC Cannot Be Claimed On

- Food and beverages, outdoor catering (unless you are in the food/hospitality business)
- Health and fitness services
- Rent-a-cab, travel, and tour packages (unless you are a travel company)
- Works contract services (for construction of immovable property)
- Goods or services for personal consumption
- Goods lost, stolen, destroyed, or given as free samples/gifts

### Why ITC Matters for Small Businesses

If you are in a business with significant purchases (manufacturing, trading, e-commerce), ITC can save you lakhs every year. This is the main reason many small businesses opt for regular registration over composition scheme — the tax savings from ITC outweigh the compliance burden.

## GST Return Filing — Schedule, Types, and Deadlines

This is where most small business owners get overwhelmed. Let us simplify.

### Returns for Regular Taxpayers

| Return | What It Contains | Due Date | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **GSTR-1** | Details of all outward supplies (sales invoices) | 11th of the next month | Monthly (turnover > ₹5 crore) |
| **GSTR-1 (QRMP)** | Same, but quarterly | 13th of the month after quarter end | Quarterly (turnover ≤ ₹5 crore) |
| **GSTR-3B** | Summary return — tax payable, ITC claimed, net tax paid | 20th of the next month | Monthly (turnover > ₹5 crore) |
| **GSTR-3B (QRMP)** | Same, but quarterly | 22nd/24th of the month after quarter end | Quarterly (turnover ≤ ₹5 crore) |
| **GSTR-9** | Annual return (consolidated summary of all monthly/quarterly returns) | 31st December of the next FY | Annual |

### Returns for Composition Scheme

| Return | What It Contains | Due Date | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CMP-08** | Summary of supplies and tax payment | 18th of the month after quarter end | Quarterly |
| **GSTR-4** | Annual return for composition taxpayers | 30th April of the next FY | Annual |

### QRMP Scheme (Quarterly Return Monthly Payment)

If your turnover is up to ₹5 crore, you can opt for the QRMP scheme:
- File GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B **quarterly** instead of monthly
- But pay tax **monthly** using **PMT-06** (by 25th of the next month)
- This reduces your filing workload from 24 returns to 8 returns per year

### Invoice Furnishing Facility (IFF)

Under QRMP, you can upload B2B invoices monthly using IFF (by 13th of the next month) so your business customers can claim ITC on time, even though you file GSTR-1 quarterly.

## Common Mistakes and Penalties

### Penalty for Late Filing

| Situation | Penalty |
|---|---|
| **Late filing of GSTR-3B** (with tax liability) | ₹50/day (₹25 CGST + ₹25 SGST), max ₹10,000 per return |
| **Late filing of GSTR-3B** (nil return) | ₹20/day (₹10 CGST + ₹10 SGST), max ₹10,000 per return |
| **Interest on unpaid tax** | 18% per annum on the tax amount, calculated from due date to payment date |
| **Late filing of GSTR-1** | ₹50/day (₹25 CGST + ₹25 SGST), max ₹10,000 |
| **Late filing of annual return (GSTR-9)** | ₹200/day (₹100 CGST + ₹100 SGST), max 0.5% of turnover |
| **Not registering when required** | Penalty equal to the tax due, or ₹10,000, whichever is higher |
| **Issuing fake invoices** | 100% penalty of the tax amount, plus possible prosecution |

### Common Mistakes Small Businesses Make

1. **Not filing nil returns** — Even if you had zero sales in a month, you must file a nil GSTR-3B and GSTR-1. Many businesses forget this and get hit with ₹20/day penalty that accumulates silently.

2. **Mixing personal and business expenses** — Claiming ITC on personal purchases (your family car, home renovation) is an invitation for scrutiny and penalties.

3. **Wrong HSN/SAC codes** — Using the wrong classification codes can result in paying the wrong tax rate. This creates mismatches during audits.

4. **Not reconciling GSTR-2B** — Your ITC claim must match what suppliers have reported in their GSTR-1. If your supplier has not filed, you lose ITC.

5. **Missing the composition scheme opt-in window** — You can switch to the composition scheme only at the start of a financial year (by filing CMP-02 before 31st March). Miss this, and you are stuck with regular GST for the whole year.

6. **Not updating GST registration** — If you change your business address, add a partner, change bank accounts, or add new business activities, you must update your registration within 15 days. Failure to do so can lead to show-cause notices.

7. **Ignoring the ₹2 lakh cash transaction limit** — Under Section 269ST of the Income Tax Act (not GST, but related), receiving ₹2 lakh or more in cash from a single person in a day is illegal. Many small businesses in cash-heavy sectors ignore this.

8. **Late payment of tax** — Paying GST a few days late attracts 18% annual interest. On a tax liability of ₹1 lakh, being 30 days late costs you ₹1,479 in interest. It adds up.

**Government fee: ₹0 (Free)**

The GST registration process on the government portal is completely free. There is no application fee, no stamp duty, and no registration charge.

| Service | Typical Cost |

| GST registration (partnership/LLP) | ₹2,000 - ₹5,000 |
| GST registration (private limited) | ₹3,000 - ₹7,000 |

**Pro tip:** Many CAs offer combo packages — registration + first year of return filing for ₹10,000-₹15,000. This is usually worth it for the peace of mind, especially if you are new to GST.

## GST for Freelancers — Special Section

Freelancers often have unique GST questions. Here is the quick summary:

- **Threshold:** ₹20 lakh (₹10 lakh in special category states) for services
- **Rate:** 18% on most professional services
- **Interstate clients:** If your clients are in other states, registration is mandatory regardless of turnover
- **Export of services:** If you work for foreign clients and get paid in foreign currency, your services may qualify as **exports** (zero-rated). You still need GST registration, but you can claim refund on GST paid on inputs
- **Reverse Charge:** If you use services from an unregistered person (say, hiring a freelance photographer who does not have GST), you may need to pay GST on their behalf under reverse charge mechanism

**Practical advice for freelancers:** If your annual billing is close to ₹20 lakh and you have interstate clients, register proactively. Getting caught without registration after crossing the threshold can result in back-taxes plus penalties.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Can I run a business without GST registration?

Yes, if your turnover is below the threshold limit (₹40 lakh for goods, ₹20 lakh for services in regular states) and you do not fall under any mandatory registration category (no interstate sales, no e-commerce). But you cannot issue GST invoices, and your business customers cannot claim ITC on purchases from you.

### 2. What happens if I cross the threshold mid-year?

You must apply for GST registration within **30 days** of crossing the threshold. The registration will be effective from the date you became liable. You must pay GST on all supplies made from that date. Late registration attracts a penalty equal to the tax due or ₹10,000, whichever is higher.

### 3. Can I cancel my GST registration?

Yes. If your turnover falls below the threshold and you are not otherwise required to be registered, you can apply for cancellation using Form GST REG-16. However, you must file all pending returns and pay any outstanding tax before cancellation. You must also reverse any ITC claimed on stock and capital goods remaining on the date of cancellation.

### 4. Is GST registration required for selling on Instagram or WhatsApp?

If you sell products through Instagram, WhatsApp, or your own website (not an e-commerce marketplace), you follow the normal threshold rules — ₹40 lakh for goods, ₹20 lakh for services. However, if you use a payment gateway that acts as an e-commerce operator, or if you sell interstate, registration may be mandatory. Get clarity from a CA based on your specific setup.

### 5. How long does GST registration take?

Typically **3-7 working days** from the date of submission, assuming all documents are in order. Aadhaar-authenticated applications may get processed faster. If the officer seeks clarification, it can take up to 30 days.

### 6. Can I have multiple GST registrations?

Yes. If you operate in multiple states, you need separate GST registration in each state. Within the same state, you can apply for multiple registrations if you have distinct business verticals (separate line items on your PAN). Each registration will have a unique GSTIN.

### 7. What is the difference between GSTIN and GST registration number?

They are the same thing. GSTIN (Goods and Services Tax Identification Number) is your 15-digit unique registration number. Some people call it GST number, GST registration number, or GSTIN — all refer to the same identifier.

### 8. Do I need GST for rental income?

Rental income from **commercial property** is taxable at 18% GST if your total turnover (including rental income) exceeds ₹20 lakh. Residential property rent was exempt, but from July 2022, if a registered person rents a residential property, GST is payable under reverse charge mechanism.

- [ ] PAN card (individual for proprietorship, firm PAN for partnership/company)
- [ ] Aadhaar card linked to mobile number for e-Sign
- [ ] Business bank account (current account preferred, savings account accepted for sole proprietors)
- [ ] Proof of business address (rent agreement + NOC, or electricity bill + property tax receipt if owned)
- [ ] Passport-size photograph (JPEG, under 100 KB)
- [ ] HSN/SAC codes for your goods/services (search on cbic-gst.gov.in)
- [ ] Digital Signature Certificate (required only for companies and LLPs)

## Wrapping Up

GST registration is not as scary as it looks. For most small businesses, the whole process takes under an hour of active work and 3-7 days for approval. The key decisions are:

1. **Do I need to register?** Check the turnover threshold and mandatory registration categories
2. **Regular or Composition?** Think about your customer base (B2B vs B2C), interstate sales, and ITC benefits
3. **Can I handle compliance?** If not, hire a CA — ₹1,000-₹3,000/month is a small price for peace of mind

Getting GST right from the start saves you from penalties, missed ITC, and compliance headaches down the road. If you are starting a new business or crossing the threshold, do not delay — register today.
