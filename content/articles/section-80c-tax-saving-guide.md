# Section 80C Tax Saving Guide — Complete List of Options for 2026

Section 80C of the Income Tax Act is the most widely used tax-saving provision in India. It allows you to claim a deduction of up to Rs 1,50,000 from your gross taxable income by investing in specified instruments. For someone in the 30% tax bracket, this translates to a tax saving of Rs 46,800 (including 4% health and education cess).

**Important:** Section 80C deductions are available only under the **old tax regime**. If you've opted for the [new tax regime](/articles/old-vs-new-tax-regime-calculator), you cannot claim 80C deductions (with limited exceptions for employer NPS contribution).

## Key Takeaways

- Maximum deduction under Section 80C: Rs 1,50,000 per financial year
- Available only under the old tax regime
- Includes investments (ELSS, PPF, NSC), insurance premiums, home loan principal, and more
- The combined limit of Rs 1.5 lakh includes Section 80C + 80CCC + 80CCD(1)
- Plan early (April) — don't rush in January-March

## Complete List of Section 80C Eligible Investments

### Market-Linked Options (Higher Return Potential)

#### 1. ELSS Mutual Funds
- **Lock-in:** 3 years (shortest among all 80C options)
- **Expected returns:** 12-18% CAGR (historically)
- **Risk:** High (equity market-linked)
- **Minimum investment:** Rs 500 (SIP)
- **Best for:** Investors with 5+ year horizon who want growth with tax saving

ELSS is our top recommendation for most investors. Read our detailed guide on the [best ELSS funds for 2026](/articles/best-elss-tax-saving-mutual-funds-2026).

#### 2. NPS (National Pension System) — under 80CCD(1)
- **Lock-in:** Until age 60 (with partial withdrawal after 3 years)
- **Expected returns:** 8-12% (depends on asset allocation)
- **Risk:** Moderate (mix of equity + debt + government bonds)
- **Minimum investment:** Rs 1,000/year
- **Additional benefit:** Rs 50,000 extra deduction under Section 80CCD(1B)

NPS gives a total deduction of up to Rs 2 lakh (Rs 1.5 lakh under 80C + Rs 50,000 under 80CCD(1B)). Read our [NPS tax benefits guide](/articles/nps-tax-benefits-section-80ccd).

### Fixed-Return Options (Safety First)

#### 3. PPF (Public Provident Fund)
- **Lock-in:** 15 years (partial withdrawal from year 7)
- **Current interest rate:** ~7.1% (government-set, revised quarterly)
- **Risk:** Zero (sovereign guarantee)
- **Investment limit:** Rs 500 to Rs 1,50,000 per year
- **Tax status:** EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) — contributions, interest, and maturity are all tax-free
- **Best for:** Risk-averse investors wanting guaranteed, tax-free returns

PPF is the gold standard for risk-free tax saving. Read our [complete PPF guide](/articles/complete-guide-ppf-public-provident-fund).

#### 4. NSC (National Savings Certificate)
- **Lock-in:** 5 years
- **Current interest rate:** ~7.7% (compounded annually, paid at maturity)
- **Risk:** Zero (government-backed)
- **Minimum investment:** Rs 1,000
- **Tax note:** Interest is taxable at slab rate (but qualifies for 80C reinvestment deduction)
- **Best for:** Risk-averse investors who want a 5-year option

#### 5. Tax-Saving Fixed Deposit (5-Year)
- **Lock-in:** 5 years (no premature withdrawal)
- **Interest rate:** 6.5-7.5% (bank-dependent)
- **Risk:** Very low (bank deposits insured up to Rs 5 lakh)
- **Minimum:** Rs 100 (varies by bank)
- **Tax note:** Interest is taxable at slab rate, TDS applies
- **Best for:** Those who want simple, familiar FDs with tax benefit

#### 6. Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (for girl child)
- **Lock-in:** 21 years or until girl's marriage after age 18
- **Current interest rate:** ~8.2% (highest among small savings)
- **Risk:** Zero (government-backed)
- **Who can open:** Parent/guardian of a girl child under 10
- **Tax status:** EEE (like PPF)
- **Maximum:** Rs 1,50,000/year
- **Best for:** Parents with daughters — excellent long-term savings

#### 7. Senior Citizen Savings Scheme (SCSS)
- **Lock-in:** 5 years (extendable by 3 years)
- **Current interest rate:** ~8.2%
- **Risk:** Zero (government-backed)
- **Who can open:** Citizens aged 60+ (55+ for voluntary retirees)
- **Maximum:** Rs 30 lakh
- **Best for:** Retirees wanting regular income with tax savings

### Insurance and Housing

#### 8. Life Insurance Premium
- **Eligible premium:** Up to 10% of sum assured (policies issued after 01/04/2012)
- **Includes:** Term insurance, endowment, ULIPs
- **Our advice:** Buy [term insurance](/articles/term-insurance-vs-whole-life-insurance) for protection, not for tax saving. Don't buy insurance as an investment.

#### 9. Home Loan Principal Repayment
- **What qualifies:** Principal portion of home loan EMI
- **Stamp duty and registration fees** also qualify in the year of purchase
- **Note:** Interest on home loan is deductible separately under Section 24(b) — up to Rs 2 lakh for self-occupied property

#### 10. Tuition Fees (for Children)
- **What qualifies:** School/college tuition fees for up to 2 children
- **Includes:** Full-time education in India
- **Excludes:** Development fees, donations, coaching fees, books
- **Per child limit:** No separate limit — included in overall Rs 1.5 lakh

#### 11. EPF (Employee Provident Fund)
- **Your contribution:** Employee's 12% contribution qualifies automatically
- **Most salaried employees** already exhaust a significant portion of 80C through EPF
- **Example:** Rs 60,000 monthly salary → Rs 7,200/month EPF → Rs 86,400/year already used from 80C limit

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Last-Minute Rush
Investing in March to meet the deadline often leads to suboptimal choices. Banks push insurance policies and FDs during this period. Start your 80C investments in April via SIPs.

### 2. Buying Insurance for Tax Saving
Endowment plans, ULIPs, and money-back policies are terrible investments. They give 4-6% returns with poor liquidity. Buy a simple [term insurance](/articles/term-insurance-vs-whole-life-insurance) for protection and invest the rest in ELSS or PPF.

### 3. Ignoring EPF Contribution
Your EPF already uses Rs 60,000-1,00,000 of the Rs 1.5 lakh limit. Calculate remaining space before making new investments.

### 4. Not Considering the New Tax Regime
If the new regime gives you lower tax without needing deductions, 80C investments for tax saving alone don't make sense. Run the numbers through our [old vs new regime calculator](/articles/old-vs-new-tax-regime-calculator).

### 5. Overlapping Investments
Don't put all Rs 1.5 lakh in one instrument. Diversify between growth (ELSS), safety (PPF), and flexibility (NPS) based on your risk profile.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I claim 80C if I chose the new tax regime?

No, Section 80C deductions are not available under the new tax regime (except employer NPS contribution under 80CCD(2), which is available in both regimes).

### Is 80C limit per person or per couple?

Per person. Both spouses can individually claim up to Rs 1.5 lakh each, giving a family total of Rs 3 lakh.

### Can I invest more than Rs 1.5 lakh in PPF?

You can only deposit up to Rs 1.5 lakh per year in PPF. Any excess won't earn interest and won't qualify for 80C.

### Do I need to submit proof of 80C investments to my employer?

Yes, usually between January-March, your employer asks for investment proof to calculate correct TDS. Submit receipts, statements, and premium receipts.

### What if I already maxed 80C through EPF and home loan?

Then you don't need additional 80C investments. Instead, invest in NPS for the Rs 50,000 extra deduction under 80CCD(1B), or focus on health insurance (80D) and other deductions.
