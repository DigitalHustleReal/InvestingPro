# Credit Card Application Rejected? What to Do Next

A credit card rejection stings, but it is not the end of the road. Understanding why you were rejected and taking corrective steps can get you approved on your next attempt. More importantly, what you do immediately after a rejection matters — the wrong moves can make things worse.

## Key Takeaways

- Do NOT apply to another bank immediately — multiple rejections compound the damage to your credit score
- The most common rejection reasons are low CIBIL score, insufficient income, and high existing debt
- Wait at least 3-6 months before your next application
- Use the waiting period to fix the specific issue that caused the rejection
- Secured credit cards (against FD) have the highest approval rates for those struggling with rejections

## Why Credit Card Applications Get Rejected

### 1. Low CIBIL Score (Most Common)

Banks typically require a CIBIL score of 700+ for standard cards and 750+ for premium cards. If your score is below the threshold, the application is auto-rejected by the bank's system before a human even reviews it.

**How to check:** Download your free CIBIL report from cibil.com and check your score. Read our guide on [how to improve your CIBIL score fast](/articles/how-to-improve-cibil-score-fast).

### 2. Insufficient Income

Every credit card has a minimum income requirement. Entry-level cards may require ₹2-3 lakh per annum, while premium cards need ₹8-15+ lakh. If your documented income does not meet the threshold, the application will be rejected.

**Common issue:** Freelancers and self-employed individuals may have higher actual income than what bank statements reflect if payments are irregular.

### 3. High Debt-to-Income Ratio

If your existing EMIs (home loan, car loan, personal loan, other credit card minimum dues) consume more than 40-50% of your monthly income, banks consider you over-leveraged.

### 4. Too Many Recent Applications

Each credit application triggers a hard inquiry on your CIBIL report. Three or more inquiries in 6 months is a red flag for lenders — it suggests you are desperately seeking credit.

### 5. Employment Instability

Banks prefer applicants with at least 1-2 years at their current employer. Recent job changes, probation periods, or gaps in employment can trigger rejection.

### 6. Errors in Application

Mismatched information between your application and KYC documents (name spelling, address, date of birth) can cause system-level rejection.

### 7. Existing Defaults or Settlements

Any "written off," "settled," or "default" status on your CIBIL report is an almost automatic rejection for the next 2-4 years.

## 8 Steps After a Credit Card Rejection

### Step 1: Do Not Panic-Apply Elsewhere

This is the most critical step. Immediately applying to another bank after rejection adds another hard inquiry to your report, further lowering your score. Each rejection makes the next application harder.

**Rule:** Wait at least 3 months, preferably 6 months, before your next credit card application.

### Step 2: Find Out the Exact Reason

Contact the bank's customer care or visit a branch. Banks are required to disclose the reason for rejection. Common responses include:
- "CIBIL score below threshold"
- "Income criteria not met"
- "High number of recent inquiries"
- "Existing obligations too high"

### Step 3: Pull Your CIBIL Report

Get your free annual CIBIL report and review:
- Your score (is it below 700?)
- Hard inquiries in the last 12 months (more than 3?)
- Any negative items (late payments, defaults, settlements)
- Errors that need disputing

### Step 4: Fix the Root Cause

| Rejection Reason | Fix | Timeline |
|-----------------|-----|----------|
| Low CIBIL score | Pay all bills on time, reduce utilization | 3-6 months |
| Insufficient income | Get salary increment documentation, add freelance income proof | 1-3 months |
| High debt ratio | Pay down existing loans/cards | 3-6 months |
| Too many inquiries | Simply wait — inquiries age off after 12 months | 6-12 months |
| Employment gap | Complete 1+ year at current job | Time-dependent |
| Errors on report | Dispute with CIBIL | 30-45 days |

### Step 5: Consider a Secured Credit Card

If your score is below 700 or income is borderline, a secured credit card against a fixed deposit is the safest path:
- Approval rates are nearly 100% since the bank has collateral
- You build credit history with responsible use
- After 12-18 months, upgrade to an unsecured card

### Step 6: Check for Pre-Approved Offers

Many banks send pre-approved credit card offers to existing customers. These do not require a hard inquiry and have very high approval rates because the bank already has your financial data.

Check:
- Your bank's mobile app (look for "offers" or "pre-approved" sections)
- Internet banking portal
- SMS/email from your primary bank

### Step 7: Apply Strategically Next Time

When you are ready to apply again:
- Choose a card where you clearly meet all eligibility criteria
- Apply to a bank where you have an existing relationship (savings account, FD, salary account)
- Have all documents ready and accurate
- Apply during salary credit week for the best account impression

### Step 8: Build a Relationship First

If multiple standalone applications have failed:
1. Open a savings account with the target bank
2. Maintain a healthy average balance for 6 months
3. Start a recurring deposit or fixed deposit
4. The bank will likely offer you a card proactively

## Alternative Options While You Wait

### Prepaid Cards with Reward Features

Services like Slice, Fi Money, and Jupiter offer card-like features with spending rewards. These do not require a CIBIL check and can serve as a bridge while you rebuild eligibility.

### Debit Card with UPI

While you wait to qualify for a credit card, use UPI for payments. Some debit cards offer basic rewards and fuel surcharge waivers.

### Add-On Card from Family

If a family member with good credit is willing, become an add-on cardholder on their existing card. This gives you a card to use and may help build your credit history.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does a credit card rejection affect my CIBIL score?

The rejection itself is not recorded on your CIBIL report. However, the hard inquiry from your application is recorded and can lower your score by 5-15 points. Multiple inquiries in a short period have a compounding negative effect.

### How long should I wait before applying again after rejection?

Wait at least 3 months, ideally 6 months. Use this time to address the rejection reason. Applying immediately to another bank will likely result in another rejection and more score damage.

### Can I appeal a credit card rejection?

Most banks do not have a formal appeal process for credit card rejections. However, if you believe the rejection was due to an error (wrong information, CIBIL report error), you can contact the bank with corrected documentation. Some banks may reconsider.

### Is a secured credit card worth it?

Absolutely, especially after a rejection. A secured credit card lets you build or rebuild credit history with minimal risk. After 12-18 months of responsible use, you can apply for a regular card with much higher chances of approval.
