Homepage Information Flow

Pattern A: Intent capture first (NerdWallet 2024)
Remove quiz gates entirely (NerdWallet did this in 2024, reducing 67% abandonment). Show all products immediately. Filter inline. Trust signals in hero, not buried in footer.
Pattern B: Score-first flow (Credit Karma, Paisabazaar)
Hero = credit score check CTA. Everything else is secondary. Once user has score, show personalised products. Highest conversion rate because intent is maximum when user has financial data in front of them.
Pattern C: Category grid (BankBazaar, Bankrate)
Homepage = icon grid of product categories. Users self-select their journey. Works for high-traffic sites where users arrive with varied intent. Poor for SEO because homepage has low topical focus.
Pattern D: Editorial + tool hybrid (MSE, InvestingPro target)
Hero = editorial statement + AI advisor widget. Trust signals embedded everywhere. Products below the fold. This is the highest-trust pattern — users feel they're talking to an advisor, not shopping in a marketplace.

5 design lessons from NerdWallet
Lesson 1
Results first, always — kill the quiz gate
NerdWallet removed their "tell us about yourself" quiz in 2024. Bounce rate dropped 67%. Show all products, let users filter themselves. Inline filters replace quiz gates.
Lesson 2
Horizontal card rows, not grid cards
Card grid = visual noise. Horizontal rows = scannable. Image on left, key info center, single CTA right. User eye tracks left-to-right naturally. Never make them hunt for the apply button.
Lesson 3
Sticky filter bar is non-negotiable
Filters must stay visible as users scroll. Users want to refine mid-scroll without returning to top. Sticky = 40% higher filter usage = better intent matching = higher conversion.
Lesson 4
Every article page embeds a calculator
No NerdWallet article is just text. Every loan article has an EMI calculator. Every mortgage article has a payment calculator. Tools = longer session = lower bounce = better Google ranking + more CTAs.
Lesson 5
Trust signals above the fold, not in footer
"How we make money" note appears above every comparison list, not buried in footer disclaimers. Users see it before they see products. Counterintuitively, this increases application rates by building credibility first.
InvestingPro Steal
Take the horizontal card row + sticky filter + tool embed pattern verbatim
These three patterns are NerdWallet's conversion backbone. Your design already incorporates these. The differentiator: add your named expert photo + approval probability ring to each row — neither of which NerdWallet has.

Groww's 5 design principles applied to InvestingPro
1. One number, big and centred. Portfolio value is always the hero number. For InvestingPro: CIBIL score or potential savings in ₹ should be the hero metric on every relevant page.
2. Charts over tables. Groww uses sparklines and charts everywhere. Data visualisation builds trust. Your SIP calculator bar chart is the right call.
3. Green = gain, always. Colour coding is non-negotiable in investment UX. Green = positive/gain/approved. Red = loss/danger/rejected. Users don't need labels when colour does the work.
4. Onboarding in under 60 seconds. Groww pioneered instant KYC for investments. Reduce friction to first value as much as possible. Your CIBIL Simulator should work without any login.
5. Bottom navigation, always visible. Groww's bottom nav is persistent. Users never lose context. For InvestingPro mobile: Home / Cards / CIBIL / Calculators / Profile.

Investopedia — the 80M-visit taxonomy masterclass
80 million monthly visits. 44 million from the glossary alone. Investopedia's IA is the deepest in finance — a taxonomy of every financial concept, each with a 2,000-word definition. The density is intentional: high dwell time = high Google ranking. Every definition page is a conversion funnel.

The glossary is a massive internal linking web that distributes link equity across the entire site and keeps users exploring within Investopedia's ecosystem.

Article Format + In-Page IA

The "Key Takeaways" box — first element on every page
Before any prose: a bulleted "Key Takeaways" box. 3–5 sentences. This is what AI models cite. This is what Google features as a snippet. This is what users skim first. Every InvestingPro article needs this.
Interactive elements embedded in every article
Real-time stock charts within articles about stocks. Calculator widgets within every financial calculation article. Video explainers for complex concepts. Avg session duration: 4+ minutes vs industry average of 2:30.
Author + Reviewer + Editor — three credential layers
Every article shows: Written by [author + credentials] → Reviewed by [expert + credentials] → Fact-checked by [editor]. Three layers of E-E-A-T. Google's quality raters see this and score it highest in YMYL. Bankrate follows the same model.

Why Saffron over Orange: BankBazaar uses orange (#FF6B00). It's generic — Swiggy, Amazon, dozens of brands use it. Saffron (#E8871A) is warmer, more editorial, distinctly Indian. No global or Indian finance platform uses it. Immediate differentiation.
Why Warm Cream over White: Pure white (#FFFFFF) is clinical — MSE, NerdWallet, and every Indian platform uses it. Warm cream (#F5F0E8) communicates "editorial authority" — premium print journalism. The FT and Economist use off-white. It signals quality without saying it.
Why Ink Navy over Black: Pure black (#000000) is harsh. Ink Navy (#1B2A4A) is deep, authoritative, trustworthy. It's the colour of a good lawyer's suit or a quality hardback book. For a financial advisor, this communicates gravitas without being cold.
