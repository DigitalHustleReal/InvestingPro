The honest constraints — what AI cannot replace
Where solo actually limits you
This plan is achievable — but only if you are honest about where AI still cannot substitute for human time, judgment, and relationships. These are the real constraints.
The 4 things that will not be automated
1. Decision-making — What to build next, which partnership to pursue, when to pivot a content strategy. AI can present options. You must decide. 2 hours per day minimum of genuine strategic thinking.

2. Relationships — The Axis Bank partnership manager will not sign a direct deal with a bot. The ET journalist will not quote an AI. The AMFI registration requires a human. Relationships require showing up as a person.

3. Legal and compliance — SEBI, RBI, AMFI, IRDA rules change. Staying compliant requires reading actual regulatory circulars and making judgment calls about your platform's positioning. Not fully automatable.

4. Creative direction — What angle to take on a story, when to publish a consumer campaign, how to position the brand. Claude is a tool. You are the editor. The editorial voice is yours to guard.
Real risk 1 — Google algorithm changes
SEO is not a guaranteed moat
Google's Helpful Content updates hurt BankBazaar in 2023. AI-generated content at scale could be penalised. Mitigation: email list is Google-proof. WhatsApp is Google-proof. Direct traffic from brand recognition is Google-proof. Build these in parallel, not after.
Real risk 2 — Competitor with funding
A funded Indian startup could copy this
Yes. But they cannot copy time. Your IFSC pages indexed in 2026 have 3 years of domain authority by 2029. Your email list built from 2026 is not copyable with money. The methodology and trust built over years cannot be bought. Start earlier, not later.
Real risk 3 — AI commoditises your content
Everyone will have AI-generated finance content
True. Which is exactly why methodology transparency, institutional trust signals, and tool quality matter so much. When 10,000 sites all produce similar AI content, the one with a published ranking methodology, AMFI badge, and a tool that actually works stands out. This is not a future problem — it is why you build the institution now.
Real risk 4 — Regulation
SEBI / RBI could restrict comparison platforms
India's financial regulators are watching platforms like yours. The protection: AMFI registration is a feature not a bug — it keeps you compliant. Publishing your methodology is a feature — it pre-empts regulatory concerns. MoneySuperMarket was regulated in the UK and got stronger. Regulation tends to hurt unregistered platforms, not registered ones.
