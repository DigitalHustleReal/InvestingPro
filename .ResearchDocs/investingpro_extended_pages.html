Most people don't realise a CIBIL of 749 vs 750 can change your home loan rate by 0.5%. On a ₹60L loan that's ₹4.8 lakh over 20 years. Read this before you apply.
"A 50-point CIBIL difference can cost you more than ₹5 lakh over the life of a home loan. The banks won't tell you that upfront — we will."
— Anjali Sharma, CFP · InvestingPro
Why your CIBIL score matters more than the bank's headline rate
Banks advertise their lowest home loan rate — say 8.35% — but that's only for borrowers with a CIBIL score above 800. If your score is 720, you'll be offered 8.85% or higher. The difference sounds small. It isn't.
On a ₹60 lakh loan over 20 years, a 0.5% rate difference means you pay approximately ₹3.8 lakh more in total interest. That's a car. A down payment on a second property. Enough to notice.

The 5 things that actually build your CIBIL score
1. Payment history (35% of your score)
This is the biggest factor — and the most actionable. Every EMI paid on time adds to your score. Even one missed payment can drop it by 50–80 points and stays on your record for 7 years. Set up auto-pay for every EMI and credit card bill today.
2. Credit utilisation (30% of your score)
If your combined credit card limit is ₹2 lakh and you're using ₹1.6 lakh of it, your utilisation is 80% — far too high. Keep it below 30%. That means if you have a ₹1 lakh limit, don't let the balance exceed ₹30,000 at any point in the billing cycle.

The honest bottom line
Improve your CIBIL score before you apply for a home loan — not after. 3 months of disciplined payoff can save you more than ₹4 lakh over 20 years.
