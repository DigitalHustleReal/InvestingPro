🏠 Home loans are the highest-value play — and most overlooked
A single ₹60L home loan at 1.5% DSA commission = ₹90,000 in one transaction. Most comparison platforms don't invest in home loan content because it's slower and lower volume than credit cards. But one home loan earns more than 25 credit card approvals. Your "how CIBIL affects your home loan rate" article already has high intent — with a good CTA and a DSA tie-up with SBI/HDFC, even 3–5 home loan leads per month transforms your income. NerdWallet's mortgage pages are their highest-revenue vertical. India's equivalent is untapped.
⚠ The conversion funnel reality — don't skip this
1,000 visitors → 8–12% click a product CTA = 80–120 clicks → 20–35% complete the application = 16–40 applications → 50–70% get approved = 8–28 commissions. At ₹1,500 average CPA: 1,000 visitors = ₹12,000–₹42,000 revenue. This is why traffic volume matters but so does intent quality. An article titled "best card for OTT subscriptions" converts 3x better than "best cashback credit cards" because the reader has already made their choice — they just need validation. High-intent long-tail = better conversion, even with less traffic.

📐 The content architecture rule — build in this exact order
Start with the pillar page for each topic cluster (e.g., "Best Credit Cards India 2026" — your homepage equivalent for the credit cards cluster). Then build cluster pages outward: cashback → travel → fuel → dining → OTT → lifetime free → by bank → by income → by credit score. This architecture means every new article you publish strengthens the pillar page's authority. Google sees a site that comprehensively covers credit cards and ranks you for everything in that space. NerdWallet did this across 9 clusters. You start with 2 (credit cards + CIBIL) and expand once those are dominant.

Technical SEO · YMYL Finance Compliance
Technical foundation — what Google actually checks for finance sites
Finance is a YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) category. Google holds it to a higher standard than any other niche. These technical and E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) requirements are non-negotiable — skipping them causes Google to suppress finance content regardless of quality.
🔐 E-E-A-T Signals (highest priority for YMYL)
Named author on every article with real credentials
Full name + photo + qualifications. Not "InvestingPro Team." Google deranks anonymous financial content. Martin Lewis built £87M on this principle.
Day 1
Author JSON-LD schema on every page
Claude Code generates: Person schema with name, jobTitle, url, sameAs (LinkedIn, Twitter). Google reads this to verify author identity.
Day 1
"Last reviewed" date on all product pages
Finance products change rates constantly. Google rewards freshness signals. Display "Last reviewed: [date]" prominently. Auto-update when rates change.
Week 1
About page with business address + contact
Google checks for real business identity on YMYL sites. Include: business address, phone, contact email, business registration number. Bankrate and NerdWallet all have this.
Week 1

Above every comparison list (not in footer). "We earn commission. It doesn't affect rankings." Linked to your methodology page. This is YMYL compliance + trust building.
